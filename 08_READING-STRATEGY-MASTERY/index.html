Why solving endless passages isn't raising your score —

01 The Big Picture — Band 9 Mindset

What Band 9 students understand that band 5/6/7 students don't. Adopt the worldview first.

Format facts you must internalise

3 sections, ~10–12 question types. The combination varies; the 3-section structure never does.
It gets progressively harder. Section 1 easiest → Section 3 hardest. The hard end-questions separate band 7 / 8 / 9.
Academic vs GT = "same but different." Different texts, but identical question types & strategies. Practise both.
Same difficulty myth-buster. GT needs 40/40 for Band 9; Academic allows 39/40. GT is not "much easier."
Computer vs paper: no score difference, but computer-based is less stressful (just click MCQs). Choose it if you can.
It's a vocabulary test too. Questions use synonyms of the text. Many "reading" problems are really vocabulary problems.

Core beliefs of a Band 9 student

  • A separate strategy for each question type — each tests different sub-skills. Band 5/6/7 students use one approach for everything; Chris has never met a Band 9 who did that.
  • Master the strategy = you could teach it with no notes, and apply it automatically on test day (like tying shoelaces).
  • It's rare to just be "bad at reading." There's always a specific reason — find it.
  • You won't understand every word — and that's fine. Your job is correct answers, not every word.
  • Don't panic at an unknown word. Ask "Do I need it? No → move on." Mindset is the big band 7 → 9 difference.
  • Strategies are a guide, not magic (the Sherpa): they guide the route, but weak reading/vocabulary still sinks you. Strategies plus real skill.
  • Tips & tricks do NOT work. No single "magic strategy for every question."
⚠️ Mindset rules
Don't cry/complain about a weakness — feel uncomfortable but keep working and convert it. "You're not bad at it — you're not good at it yet." Success leaves clues: copy what Band 9 students all do. And you improve by action, not by watching one more YouTube video.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
প্রতিটা question type আলাদা — আলাদা strategy লাগবে, আর এমনভাবে রপ্ত করতে হবে যেন পরীক্ষায় চিন্তা না করেই প্রয়োগ হয়। সব শব্দ বোঝা লাগবে না; কাজ হলো সর্বোচ্চ সঠিক উত্তর। "Trick" বলে কিছু নেই — strategy + আসল reading skill + vocabulary একসাথে।

02 The 4-Tire System

Chris's answer to "Why isn't my score increasing no matter how much I work?" — this is probably you.

🚗 The car with 4 tires
A $100,000 Tesla with one busted tire is useless. The 4 tires below are all required, in order. Miss one → no matter how many tests/courses you do, your score will not rise. Worse: practising the wrong things makes you worse.

🛞 Tire 1 — A strategy per question type

Different types test different sub-skills. Goal isn't to see a strategy once — it's to master it (teach-with-no-notes → automatic). An un-mastered strategy = a broken tire.

🛞 Tire 2 — Practice tests, used right

Goal 1: master strategies — take as long as you want (time doesn't matter). Goal 2: once comfortable, do tests under exam conditions to surface weaknesses.

🛞 Tire 3 — Weakness analysis

Think deeply about each mistake → find the pattern (e.g. 8 of 10 wrong = spelling). Skip this = busted tire.

🛞 Tire 4 — Work on weaknesses

Knowing isn't enough (students told the exact fix still did nothing → failed). 🎯 Sniper, not shotgun — fix the 1–2 things actually holding you back.
⏳ The 5th part — TIME (the binding rule)
"Just give me quick tips!" → No. There are no quick tips. Pharmacist: you can't demand the headache cure work in 5 minutes. Two students (both start June 1): the one who rushes everything into 30 days to hit a booked date fails; the one who just focuses on step 1→2→3→4 gets the score fastest. Flat tire takes 30 min to change properly — demand 5 and you go nowhere.
✅ Recap: ① get strategies that work → master them; ② weakness analysis on practice tests; ③ work on those weaknesses; ④ respect time. Miss one → you fail. All four in order → great results.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
গাড়ির ৪টা চাকা — (১) প্রতিটা type-এর strategy রপ্ত, (২) practice test দুইভাবে (আগে strategy রপ্ত করতে, পরে exam condition-এ দুর্বলতা খুঁজতে), (৩) ভুলের প্যাটার্ন বিশ্লেষণ, (৪) সেই দুর্বলতা ঠিক করা (sniper)। একটা চাকা নষ্ট = গাড়ি চলবে না। আর সবকিছুর জন্য সময় দিতে হবে — শর্টকাট নেই।

03 The Ultimate Band 9 Workflow

The 4-Tire idea as a repeatable 5-step loop you run on every real test.

🩺 Doctor
Diagnose first, then treat. Tests are for diagnosis, not endless re-testing.
💪 Gym
Muscles grow after the gym. Improvement comes after practice, from the analysis.
🚜 Farm driver
7 yrs driving daily, failed repeatedly; 6 wks proper practice passed. Mindless practice entrenches bad habits.

The 5 steps

  1. Get REAL practice tests (never fake) — Cambridge.org, ielts.org, IDP, British Council, Cambridge books. Two or three you've never seen.
  2. Do them under EXAM CONDITIONS — quiet room, exact 60 minutes, no peeking, don't go 30 seconds over.
  3. HONEST correction — one letter wrong = wrong. No gifting yourself marks.
  4. Ask WHY you got each wrong (most important) — write the reason for every ✗, look for patterns. An investment of time, not wasted time.
  5. WORK ON THE WEAKNESS — turn it into a strength. Biggest weakness first (80/20: fixing it can kill 80–90% of mistakes).

Then loop: new test → diagnose new weaknesses → fix → repeat.

📏 The readiness rule — when may you book the test?
Book only when you score half a band above target, three times in a row. Need Band 8 → hit 8.5+ ×3. If you get 7.5/8/7.5, you'll likely get 7–7.5 on the day (stress, not harder tests). The #1 cause of failure (>99%): booking before ready.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
৫ ধাপ — (১) আসল test জোগাড়, (২) exam condition-এ করা, (৩) সৎ correction (এক অক্ষর ভুল = ভুল), (৪) প্রতিটা ভুলের কারণ লিখে প্যাটার্ন বের করা (সবচেয়ে গুরুত্বপূর্ণ), (৫) সেই দুর্বলতা শক্তিতে রূপান্তর — বড় দুর্বলতা আগে। target-এর half band বেশি টানা ৩ বার পেলে তবেই বুক করো।

04 Weakness Analysis — Read Your Wrong Answers

The one technique 95% of students don't know. Diagnose only from the questions you got wrong.

The student case-studies — which one are you?

Student A — wrong only on two question types (e.g. every TFNG + every Matching Headings). → No reading/vocab problem; just needs strategies for those two types.
Student B — wrong cluster in Section 3. → Reading skills and/or vocabulary (harder texts). Could be a timing issue.
Student C — found the right answer but spelled it wrong. → Pure spelling. (One student went Band 5 → 8 fixing only this.)
Student D — wrong cluster on matching types. → Usually a scanning sub-skill issue ("I get lost / run out of time" = can't scan).

The 5 mistake categories (master checklist)

#CategoryHow to spot itHow to fix it
1SpellingRight word, wrong letters.Log misspellings + correct spelling → 80/20 patterns. Active reading.
2Not reading instructionsToo many words; wrong format.A cognitive habit — deliberately practise reading instructions every time.
3Reading skillsCan't locate (scan) / can't get gist (skim) / can't decide in the paragraph (close read).Identify the weak sub-skill & drill it.
4VocabularyText feels hard, you get lost.No shortcut — active reading + notebook + synonyms over time. Biggest 7→9 gap.
5A particular question typeConsistently fail one type across tests.Learn its strategy → practise slowly → speed up.
🎯 Prioritisation
Fix your biggest weakness first. Hardest, but it makes everything else far easier.
⚠️ A teacher can't fix this for you
Reading is a receiving skill — a teacher can't see your thought process. Self-diagnosis from your wrong answers is more effective than even a teacher beside you.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
শুধু ভুল উত্তর বিশ্লেষণ করো। প্রতিটা ভুলকে ৫ ক্যাটাগরির একটায় ফেলো — বানান / instruction / reading skill / vocabulary / নির্দিষ্ট question type। প্যাটার্ন বের করে বড় দুর্বলতা আগে ঠিক করো। reading-এ teacher-এর feedback সীমিত — নিজের self-diagnosis-ই সেরা।

05 Core Reading Skills

Strategies just tell you which skill to use, when, and in what order. Here are the skills.

📰 Skim

Read fast for general meaning (like a newspaper). Key skill for Matching Headings.

🎬 Scan

Search for specific info / location (like cinema times). Find where the answer is — not the answer.

🔬 Close reading

Read carefully around the location to decide the answer. Most important & most overlooked — Band 9 students spend most time here.
🧭 The golden principle — "Where before what"
First find WHERE the answer is (scan), then decide WHAT it is (close read). You focus on one paragraph instead of reading all 6–7.

Active vs passive reading

Passive = reading for enjoyment only → near-zero IELTS improvement.
Active = reading while working on vocabulary / grammar / spelling / skills: guess unknown words from context then look them up; write words in a notebook with synonyms; notice punctuation.
⚡ Power line
"10 minutes of active reading beats 1 hour of passive reading." And: anyone expecting Band 9 without reading is "insane" — read things you enjoy so it's sustainable.

Vocabulary & coping with unknown words

  • The reading test is a vocabulary test — synonyms are how you locate answers. A "time problem" is often a vocabulary problem.
  • Guess meaning from context, then confirm — you'll be right 95%+ (this is how natives read).
  • Don't panic — ask "do I need this word?" Usually no → move on.
  • Best way to build vocabulary = reading (active + notebook). The range of vocabulary is the big 7→9 difference.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
৩টা skill — Skim (দ্রুত general meaning), Scan (উত্তরের জায়গা খোঁজা), Close reading (ওই জায়গা পড়ে উত্তর ঠিক করা — সবচেয়ে গুরুত্বপূর্ণ)। নিয়ম: "Where before what"। Active reading করো — ১০ মিনিট active = ১ ঘণ্টা passive। অজানা শব্দে ভয় নয়, context থেকে আন্দাজ করো।

06 All 12 Question Types

Each = key problem(s) + step-by-step. Two rules run through all: "where before what" & synonym awareness. Click any type to expand.

⚠️ Don't skip
Never assume the types are "all the same." Each tests a different sub-skill. Order tricks: Matching Headings → do FIRST; Matching Information → do LAST; Section 1 → do fast.
1 Sentence Completion ▸

Key problems: not reading instructions (word count); spelling (you write the word).

  1. Read instructions (word limit).
  2. Read the incomplete sentences first (not the text).
  3. Predict the answer type/content.
  4. Think of synonyms.
  5. Scan for location.
  6. Close-read & decide.
  7. Check spelling.
2 Summary Completion ▸

Key problems: instructions/word count; spelling; grammar matters more (noun/verb/adjective?).

  1. Read instructions.
  2. Read the summary first.
  3. Predict content and word type (grammar makes the answer obvious).
  4. Scan for location; synonyms; "where before what".
  5. Close-read; check it makes sense.
  6. Decide & insert.
3 Multiple Choice (MCQ) ▸

Key problem: not understanding the difference between options.

  1. Read question + options carefully first; understand differences.
  2. Skim the text (general meaning).
  3. Focus on option differences (most important).
  4. Locate the section (synonyms).
  5. Close-read.
  6. Confused? Eliminate the definitely-wrong → compare the 2 left → check text.
  7. Re-read question; final choice.
4 Short Answer Questions RARE ▸

Key problems: rushing / misreading the question; not understanding words in the text.

  1. Read & understand the questions first.
  2. Underline keywords (usually nouns).
  3. Think synonyms for those nouns.
  4. Scan for location.
  5. Close-read.
  6. Re-read the question (your big risk).
  7. Decide & write (word count + spelling).
5 Labelling a Diagram / Map ▸

Key problems: can't cope with the unfamiliar (they test interpreting an unfamiliar diagram via the text — not topic knowledge); instructions/limit.

  1. Read instructions (word limit).
  2. Study the diagram — don't panic.
  3. Highlight keywords on it.
  4. Look at the gaps; predict.
  5. Scan to find location.
  6. Read in detail; choose.
  7. Check spelling.
6 True / False / Not Given DEEP DIVE → §07 ▸

About facts. Key problems: online misinformation; not knowing what "Not Given" means; wasting time hunting what isn't there; over-focusing on keywords vs whole sentence.

  1. Read instructions (confirm TFNG not YNNG).
  2. Read the whole statement; note qualifying words.
  3. Think synonyms.
  4. Match to location.
  5. Re-read statement; close-read section.
  6. Decide: matches→True, contradicts→False, not there→Not Given.
7 Yes / No / Not Given DEEP DIVE → §07 ▸

Same as TFNG but about the writer's OPINION. Key problems: confusing fact (TFNG) vs opinion (YNNG); not grasping the writer's opinion; confusing the writer's opinion with other people's.

Strategy = identical to TFNG, focused on the writer's view: Yes = agrees, No = disagrees, Not Given = no info on the writer's opinion.

8 Matching Sentence Endings RARE ▸

Key problem: using your own logic/grammar instead of the text.

  1. Read the sentence beginnings first.
  2. Predict endings before looking.
  3. Read endings; match the obvious ones.
  4. Eliminate obviously wrong; shortlist 1–2.
  5. Locate, close-read, choose — based on the TEXT.
9 Matching Names ▸

Often research findings, toward the end. Key problem: matching names fast without reading detail (tired/rushed).

  1. Scan & underline each name in the text.
  2. Start with names mentioned only once (small area → fast).
  3. Read that part in detail; match.
  4. Delete the matched statement.
  5. Move to next single-mention name; then twice-mentioned; repeat.
10 Matching Information ("which paragraph…") ▸

Key problem: must consider the whole text.

  1. Do this LAST in the section (skip if it appears first).
  2. Read questions first; synonyms.
  3. Skim the whole text.
  4. Re-read questions; think location (some obvious).
  5. Scan & underline.
  6. Mark correct if you can.
  7. Stuck? Move on — the answer often appears in the next paragraph.
11 Table / Flowchart Completion ▸

Table = more common in Academic; flowchart common in GT. Key problem: not reading instructions (word/number limit).

  1. Read instructions carefully.
  2. Scan to locate the correct paragraph.
  3. Read carefully (don't just skim).
  4. Transfer words EXACTLY.
  5. Check spelling (you're copying — "no excuse").
12 Matching Headings DEEP DIVE → §08 ▸

Always more headings than paragraphs. Tests the general meaning of each paragraph. Key problems: too much info; reading only first/last sentence or keywords; not differentiating similar headings.

  1. Do this FIRST in the section.
  2. Ignore the headings at first.
  3. Read each paragraph & write your own heading.
  4. Then read the headings; understand differences.
  5. Match the obvious ones.
  6. Close-read the rest; move on if stuck.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
প্রতিটা type আলাদা sub-skill টেস্ট করে। প্রায় সব ক্ষেত্রে: আগে প্রশ্ন পড়ো, synonym ভাবো, "আগে কোথায়, পরে কী", তারপর close reading। Completion/diagram/table-এ word limit + বানান। Matching Headings আগে, Matching Information শেষে। আটকে গেলে confidence নিয়ে পরেরটায় যাও।

07 TFNG & YNNG — Masterclass

The most-feared type. Full VIP treatment + the personalisation technique.

The 4 key problems (TFNG)

  • Misinformation — most online teachers aren't examiners.
  • Not knowing what "Not Given" means → hunting for something that isn't there → wrecks other questions too.
  • Failing to understand the whole sentence (the keyword trap).
  • Getting lost in the text wasting time.

Tips before the strategy

1. Ignore outside knowledge / common sense. Use only the text. You can't get Band 9 "without reading the text."
2. Spot QUALIFYING words — some vs all, many, female vs male polar bear. They test whole-sentence understanding.
3. There's always ≥1 True, ≥1 False, ≥1 Not Given. Deduce the leftover if confident on the rest.
4. Don't rely on skim/scan — the main skill is close reading (skim/scan are its opposite).
5. Be aware of synonyms — bone → skeleton / skeletal system.
6. Can't find it? Write "NG" and move on; re-check later. It's probably Not Given.
🧠 The personalisation technique — kills "Not Given" confusion
Practise with facts about yourself. Statement = "Chris is 40." → True: "born 16 Dec 1982" (agrees). False: "Chris is 21" (contradicts). Not Given: "Chris is middle-aged" (related, but not enough info — could be 40 or 55). Second: nationality. "married to a Vietnamese woman, sons have Vietnamese passports" → is he Vietnamese? We don't know → Not Given. Never import outside knowledge. Do it 2–3× → on test day anchor to a fact about yourself.
✅ Decision logic (keep it simple)
First: is it there at all? No → Not Given. If there: meaning matches → True; you can correct/contradict it → False. (Trick: if you can correct the statement to make it true, it's False.)

Worked example 🐻

"Polar bears suffer from various health problems due to fat under their skin." → text: humans with that much fat would be obese/diabetic, "yet the polar bear experiences no such consequences." Statement says suffer; text says no consequences → FALSE.

Yes / No / Not Given

Same format, about the writer's opinion. Extra trap: confusing the writer's opinion with other people's (if only "Jason" says it and the writer doesn't comment → Not Given). Strategy = TFNG, focused on the writer.
🎓 Band 9 students confirm
Shrubani & Priyanka both stopped reading the passage blindly and started comparing paragraph vs statement; the repeated mantra: develop the confidence to write "Not Given" and move on. A short clip ≠ mastery — needs slow practice until automatic.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
TFNG = তথ্য; YNNG = লেখকের মতামত — গুলিও না। নিয়ম: আগে দেখো তথ্য আছে কিনা — না থাকলে Not Given; থাকলে মিললে True, বিরোধ করলে (বা statement "শুদ্ধ" করা গেলে) False। শুধু keyword নয়, পুরো বাক্যের মানে; qualifying words খেয়াল; বাইরের জ্ঞান নয়। নিজের সম্পর্কে fact দিয়ে অনুশীলন করো। সবচেয়ে বড় শিক্ষা: "Not Given" লেখার সাহস।

08 Matching Headings — Masterclass

Harder than TFNG for most — but easy with one transformative move. This single tip has taken hundreds of students from ~6→8 or ~7→9.

What it tests: the general meaning of each whole paragraph (NOT keywords or single sentences) + differentiating similar headings.
Common problems: too much info; reading only first/last sentence or keywords; not understanding heading differences; missing the general meaning.
⭐ The transformative technique — "write your OWN heading first"
You can't title a paragraph without understanding it — so writing your own heading forces you to do exactly what's tested.
  1. Do this question FIRST in its section (you read the whole text anyway → other questions get easier).
  2. DO NOT look at the given headings first (looking first → confusion).
  3. Read each paragraph & write YOUR OWN heading (carefully, not skimming too fast). If a section is 2–3 paragraphs, write ONE heading for the main idea.
  4. Then look at the headings; understand the differences in meaning.
  5. Match the obvious ones — yours often matches a given heading exactly/nearly → correct.
  6. For the rest: close-read & compare meaning differences. Cover others with a ruler, or write the 2 similar ones side by side.
  7. If stuck: guess & come back (don't burn 5–10 min).

Worked example 🤖 (AI passage, Q27)

3 paragraphs: AI benefits → AI better than humans but distrusted → why distrust. Your single heading: "AI is better at many things than humans." Match → "The superiority of AI projections over those made by humans" ✅. Traps ("people don't like relying on AI") are details, not the paragraph's general meaning.
🎓 Band 9 students confirm
Priyanka, Jaswant & Shrubani all said: don't read headings first — write your own, then match. Jaswant credited this one tip for a "perfect" reading score; Shrubani stressed slow practice + memorising the strategy.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
আগে দেওয়া heading দেখবে না। প্রতিটা paragraph পড়ে নিজে একটা heading লেখো (এতে বাধ্য হয়ে general meaning বুঝবে — এটাই টেস্ট)। তারপর heading মিলিয়ে দেখো — হুবহু/প্রায় মিললে সেটাই উত্তর। এই type section-এ আগে করো। keyword/প্রথম-শেষ লাইনে আটকে থেকো না।

09 The Time-Management Truth

The most counter-intuitive lesson: time-management "tips" don't exist.

🏃 The Usain Bolt principle
Runners behind Bolt don't lose on time management — Bolt is just better at running. He didn't Google "time tips" the night before — he spent years getting faster. You finish on time because you're better at reading (skills + vocabulary + strategy + practice), not because of tricks. (Chris got Band 9 in 20 minutes — he's a native-speaker IELTS teacher.)

⚙️ Speed = automaticity

Master a strategy per type until it's automatic (like riding a bike). That speed comes from mastery + reduces stress & cognitive load.

⏱️ The one real time-habit: section pacing

Sections get harder (1→3). Don't spend equal time. Do Section 1 fast & accurate, bank time for Section 3. (Jaswant: Sections 1–2 in ~30 min. Shrubani: tracked section times in Excel, drove Section-1 time 10→7→5 min.)
🐢→🐇 Slow then fast
Beginners: practise extremely slowly (even 30–60 min for 6 TFNG) — goal is perfecting the strategy, not speed. Then build up: 3–4 hrs → 2.5 → 2 → 1.5 → 1h15 → 1h05 → 60 min. (Driving lesson 1 isn't at 100 mph.)
⚠️ The trap
A "time problem" is often a vocabulary problem (can't find words fast enough). Fix vocabulary & the speed follows.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়

12 Lessons from the Live Demo Test

The strategies in action — concrete, reusable lessons from the worked walkthrough.

🔧 Pre-flight move (every section)
First check which question types are in the section. If Matching Headings is present, do it first; Matching Information last; else dive in.

🌞 Part 1 — Diagram + Sentence Completion + TFNG

  • Diagram: study it first; a wrong prediction is fine; arrows/visual details confirm the location (the up/down arrows matched "hot material rises and plunges").
  • Completion: predict by grammar & eliminate ungrammatical options — grammar reveals the answer even if you don't know the science (plasma/helium). Synonyms decide it (fraction = small proportion; sunburn = skin damage).
  • TFNG: hinges on qualifying words — "only two elements" vs "almost entirely" → False. Where text never compares/quantifies ("most of the surface", light speed) → Not Given, not False.

🌊 Part 2 — Matching Headings + Short Answer + MCQ

  • Matching Headings first; self-headings matched given ones near-exactly → delete used headings → eliminate.
  • Vocabulary decisive: not knowing "dairy industry" → wrong answer. Trim to word limit ("high levels of water pollution" → "water pollution").
  • MCQ trap: "as big as Texas" appears, but text says "some have theorised" (theory ≠ fact) → answer is "of unknown size." Keywords give location, not the answer.

🗺️ Part 3 — Table + Matching Information + Matching Names

  • Table: mind word limit ("the king and queen"); confirm "he" = Columbus before writing.
  • Matching Info: "rebuffed" = refused (guess from context); move on if not found.
  • Matching Names: start with once-mentioned names (Pizarro); use elimination for the heavily-mentioned (Columbus).
Two big takeaways: ① strategy + correct order saves huge time; ② it's reading + vocabulary AND being smart (strategy/order) — together.
🇧🇩 বাংলায়
প্রতি section-এ আগে type চেক করো (Matching Headings আগে)। diagram আগে বোঝো। completion-এ grammar দিয়ে predict ও eliminate; synonym-ই উত্তর মেলায়। TFNG-তে qualifying words; text তুলনা/পরিমাণ না দিলে Not Given। MCQ-তে keyword শুধু জায়গা দেয় ("some theorise" ≠ "is known to")।
