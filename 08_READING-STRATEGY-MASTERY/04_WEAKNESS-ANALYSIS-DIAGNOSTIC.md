# 04 — WEAKNESS ANALYSIS: The Diagnostic (read your wrong answers)

> *"The clue to improving to Band 9 is looking at your answers."* This is the single technique 95% of students don't even know about. You **diagnose from the questions you got WRONG**, never the ones you got right.

---

## THE PROCEDURE
1. Use **official** practice tests (do **at least one**, ideally **two or three**) under **exam conditions**.
2. Focus **only on the questions you got wrong.**
3. For each wrong answer, work out the **root cause**, and look for a **pattern** across the test(s).
4. Turn the dominant weakness into a strength.

---

## 🧑‍🎓 THE STUDENT CASE-STUDIES (map yourself onto one)
Chris demonstrates that *every student is different* — the same low score has different causes:

- **Student A — wrong answers cluster on TWO question types** (e.g. every **True/False/Not Given** and every **Matching Headings**), everything else correct (incl. hard Section 3). → **No** reading/vocab problem. They just need a **strategy for those two question types.** Fix two things → score jumps.

- **Student B — wrong answers cluster in SECTION 3** (Sections 1–2 mostly correct). → Indicates **reading skills and/or vocabulary** (Section 3 texts & words are harder; needs more skimming/scanning/close reading). *Could also be a* **timing** *issue* (fine early, ran out of time) → fix via question-type familiarity + strategy, then build speed.

- **Student C — got the correct answer but spelled it wrong** (equipment, concerns, procedure, injuries, sunlight, transpiration, weight…). → **NOT** strategy, speed, vocabulary, or reading skills. **Pure spelling.** (One student jumped **Band 5 → Band 8** just by fixing spelling.)

- **Student D — wrong answers cluster on MATCHING questions** (matching information, matching headings, matching statements). → A **matching** problem → usually a **scanning** sub-skill issue ("I get lost / run out of time" is really *can't scan*). May also be **skimming** or **close reading**.

> **Lesson:** Without this analysis, a student might do a long expensive reading course and **still not improve**, because their real issue (e.g. spelling) was never addressed.

---

## 🎯 THE 5 KEY MISTAKE CATEGORIES (the master checklist)
For every wrong answer, classify it into ONE of these:

| # | Category | How to spot it | How to fix it |
|---|----------|----------------|---------------|
| **1** | **Spelling** | You found the right word but spelled it wrong (even 1 letter). | Note every misspelling + the correct spelling beside it → find patterns of words/types you misspell → 80/20 fix. Improve via **active reading**. |
| **2** | **Not reading instructions** | Wrote 3 words when max was 2; wrote a word when it asked for a letter, etc. | It's a **cognitive skill** — practise reading instructions carefully, *every* question, repeatedly. You can't "just remember" under stress. |
| **3** | **Reading/Listening skills** | Struggle to **find the location** (= scanning) / struggle with **general meaning** (= skimming) / struggle to decide the answer in the located paragraph (= **reading for detail / close reading**). | Identify which sub-skill; drill it. Use the strategy files — where a strategy says "skim/scan/read for detail" and you're weak there, that's your gap. |
| **4** | **Vocabulary** | Text feels "difficult", you get lost, can't keep up, don't understand words / differences between words. | **No magic fix, no shortcut word-list** — build vocabulary over time (active reading + vocabulary notebook + synonyms). Biggest single gap between Band 7 and Band 9. |
| **5** | **Particular question type** | Across 2–3 tests, you consistently fail one type (e.g. all Yes/No/Not Given wrong). Not a vocab/skill/spelling issue — you're just **not comfortable with that type yet.** | Learn its strategy → practise it **slowly** → get comfortable → speed up. |

---

## ⭐ PRIORITISATION RULE
> **Focus on your BIGGEST weakness first.** It's the hardest, but clearing it makes everything else much easier.

## ⚠️ WHAT DOES *NOT* WORK (explicitly)
- A teacher giving you direct feedback on reading is **limited** — unlike writing/speaking (where you *produce* language a teacher can correct), reading/listening are **receiving** skills; the teacher "would have to go into your brain." So **self-diagnosis from your wrong answers is more effective than even a teacher sitting beside you.**
- Watching more YouTube. Tips/tricks/hacks. Just "doing more tests."

🇧🇩 **বাংলায়:** শুধু **ভুল উত্তরগুলো** বিশ্লেষণ করো। প্রতিটা ভুলকে ৫টা ক্যাটাগরির একটায় ফেলো — (১) বানান, (২) instruction না পড়া, (৩) reading skill (skim/scan/close reading), (৪) vocabulary, (৫) নির্দিষ্ট question type-এ দুর্বলতা। প্যাটার্ন বের করে **সবচেয়ে বড় দুর্বলতা আগে** ঠিক করো। reading-এ teacher-এর feedback সীমিত — কারণ এটা "receiving" skill; তাই নিজের self-diagnosis-ই সবচেয়ে কার্যকর।
