# 10 — PRACTICE PRINCIPLES: Real vs Fake Tests, Slow vs Fast, Readiness

> How Band 9 students use practice tests *differently*. "Every single minute a Band 9 student is using a practice test, they are improving." Practice, done wrong, makes you **worse**, not better.

---

## 📍 WHERE TO GET REAL TESTS (and only here)
**The only reliable sources** (everything else is fake/unreliable):
1. **Cambridge.org**
2. **ielts.org**
3. **IDP**
4. **British Council**
5. **The Cambridge IELTS books** (the past-paper series)

- For the first four: Google **"IELTS reading practice test" + "Cambridge"/"ielts.org"/"IDP"/"British Council"** → free official tests.
- The Cambridge books cost ~**$20–30** but are effectively **free / an investment**: failing the test wastes ~**$200–300 per attempt** (the average student spends up to ~$1,000 over 3 attempts). Spending $25 to avoid that is "one of the best investments you can make." (Chris has **no affiliation** with Cambridge.)
- ❌ **Do NOT download "free soft-copy" PDFs** — the vast majority (90–95%) are **fake and misleading** → lead to failure. Don't trust sites named "realgenuineIELTStests.com" / "wepromisewearenotfake.com." A `.com` claiming to be real doesn't make it real.

### Why fake tests are dangerous
Made by people who aren't IELTS experts (it takes **decades** to produce reliable tests), only to **make money**. They're either:
- **Too easy** → you feel ready, pay your fee, **fail.**
- **Too hard** → you lose confidence, get stressed, **give up** / never book.

---

## 🧪 THE 5 KEY PRACTICE PRINCIPLES

1. **Practice does NOT make perfect.** (The 7-years-on-the-farm driver who failed repeatedly vs the 6-weeks-of-proper-practice friends who passed.) Mindless practice **entrenches bad habits** → you get worse.
2. **Most practice tests are fake** (only the 5 sources above are safe).
3. **Improvement comes AFTER practice** (gym analogy — muscles grow after the gym, not during; over-doing it shrinks them). It's what you do **with the results** that improves you.
4. **The real goal of practice is to ESTABLISH WEAKNESSES**, not to "get better" by repetition (doctor analogy — diagnose, then treat; don't keep running tests).
5. **Understand SLOW practice vs FAST practice** (below).

---

## 🐢 SLOW PRACTICE vs 🐇 FAST PRACTICE
- **SLOW practice** = when **learning a strategy / fixing a weakness.** Go very slowly — 20/30/45 min for 6 TFNG questions is fine. **Goal = mastering the strategy, NOT speed or even the correct answer.** (Driving lesson 1 isn't done at 100 mph.) Then speed up gradually.
- **FAST practice** = when **establishing your weaknesses / checking readiness.** Done **under exam conditions.**

### Rules for FAST (exam-condition) practice
1. **Don't cheat** — never use questions you've seen before; don't flick through the book first.
2. **Exam conditions** — quiet room, no distractions, **exact official time**; don't go even 30 seconds over.
3. **Honest correction** — spelled wrong = **wrong**. No "I'll do better on the day."
4. **Establish your real score / band** — count correct, convert to band (Google "X out of 40 in band score" or use a converter).

---

## 📏 THE READINESS RULE (when to book the test)
- **Book only when you score half a band above your target, three times in a row.**
- e.g. need **Band 8** → get **8.5+ three consecutive times.**
- If you need 8 but get 7.5 / 8 / 7.5 → you'll likely get **7–7.5 on the day** (stress, not harder tests).
- **#1 cause of failure (>99%):** booking **before ready.** It's far cheaper to over-prepare than to fail 2–3 times.

---

## 🩹 MATCHING WEAKNESS → FIX (the practice routing table)
After fast practice + analysis, route each weakness to its fix:
- **Spelling** → note misspellings + correct spelling; active reading; find patterns (80/20).
- **Not reading instructions** → deliberately practise reading instructions every time (a cognitive habit, can't be willed under stress).
- **Reading/listening skills** → drill the weak sub-skill (skim/scan/close reading) using the strategy guides.
- **Vocabulary** → no shortcut; active reading + vocabulary notebook + a vocabulary-improvement plan over time.
- **A specific question type** → learn its strategy, practise slowly, get comfortable, speed up.
- **Always start with your BIGGEST weakness first.**
