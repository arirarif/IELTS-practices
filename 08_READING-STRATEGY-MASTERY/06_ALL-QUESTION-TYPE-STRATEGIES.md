# 06 — ALL QUESTION TYPES: Step-by-Step Strategies + Common Problems

> The complete set of question types Chris covers (≈10–12 types). Each entry = **Key Problem(s)** + **Step-by-Step Strategy**. Two recurring rules run through *all* of them: **"where before what"** (find location before deciding the answer) and **be aware of synonyms.**
>
> ⚠️ **Do NOT assume the first few types are "all the same" and skip.** A huge mistake — each tests a *different* sub-skill.
>
> 🔎 The two hardest types get their own deep-dive files: **TFNG/YNNG → `07`**, **Matching Headings → `08`**. They're summarised here too for a single reference.

---

## 1) SENTENCE COMPLETION
Complete incomplete sentences using **word(s) from the text**.
**Key problems:** (a) not reading instructions (esp. **word count**); (b) **spelling** (you write the word, so one wrong letter = wrong).
**Strategy:**
1. **Read the instructions carefully** (word limit!).
2. **Read the incomplete sentences FIRST** (not the text). Understand them.
3. **Predict** the answers — what *type* of word / what content is likely. (Even wrong predictions "prime your brain.")
4. Think of **synonyms** of your predictions.
5. **Scan for the LOCATION** ("where before what").
6. **Read that section carefully**, decide the answer.
7. **Check spelling.**

## 2) SUMMARY COMPLETION
Complete a summary (e.g. "no more than two words") using words from the text. *Very similar to sentence completion*, but **grammar matters more.**
**Key problems:** not reading instructions (word count); spelling; **grammar** (is the gap a noun/verb/adjective?).
**Strategy:**
1. Read instructions carefully.
2. **Read the summary text first** (imperative — before the text).
3. **Predict** content **and word type** (noun? verb? adjective?). Strong grammar makes the answer obvious.
4. Scan for **location**; be aware of synonyms; remember **where before what**.
5. Read the section carefully; **check the answer makes sense** (e.g. if only an adjective fits, a noun is wrong → re-check).
6. Decide & insert.

## 3) MULTIPLE CHOICE (MCQ)
Direct question + 3–4 options; pick the correct one.
**Key problem:** not understanding the **difference between the options** (slight variations in meaning).
**Strategy:**
1. **Read the question + options carefully FIRST**; understand the **differences** between options.
2. **Skim** the text (read quickly for general meaning — *don't* read in detail).
3. **Focus on the difference between the options** (stated twice — it's the most important thing).
4. For each question, **locate** the relevant section (be aware of synonyms) — find location, not answer yet.
5. **Close-read** that section.
6. If confused: think **which options are definitely WRONG** → eliminate → focus on the 2 remaining → find the big difference → check the text.
7. Read the question again; make the final choice.

## 4) SHORT ANSWER QUESTIONS *(rare, but be ready)*
Direct comprehension questions (like school) with a direct answer.
**Key problems:** rushing / not understanding the **question**; not understanding many **words/phrases** in the text.
**Strategy:**
1. **Read & understand the questions first** (don't get lost in the text).
2. **Underline keywords** in the questions (usually **nouns / noun phrases** — people, places, objects).
3. Think of **synonyms** for those nouns → helps you scan.
4. **Scan for the location** ("where before what").
5. **Read the section carefully.**
6. **Read the question again** (your big risk is misreading the question).
7. Decide & write it in (mind the word count + spelling).

## 5) LABELLING A DIAGRAM (or MAP) *(usually a diagram)*
Diagram with arrows to parts you must label.
**Key problems:** (a) **inability to cope with the unfamiliar** (e.g. a diagram of a ship you know nothing about — they're testing if you can **interpret an unfamiliar diagram via the text**, not your topic knowledge — because you'll do this daily in your new country); (b) not reading instructions (word/number limit).
**Strategy:**
1. **Read instructions carefully** (word limit).
2. **Study the diagram to understand it — don't panic.**
3. **Highlight keywords** on the diagram.
4. Look at the **gaps/spaces** you must fill; **predict** the answers.
5. **Scan to find the location.**
6. **Read in detail**, choose the answer.
7. **Check spelling.**
> *(See `12_DEMO-WALKTHROUGH-LESSONS.md` for the worked "layers of the Sun" diagram — studying the diagram first prevents misconceptions.)*

## 6) TRUE / FALSE / NOT GIVEN  → **deep dive in `07`**
Decide if statements are True / False / Not Given **about the facts** in the text.
**Key problems:** misinformation online; not understanding **what "Not Given" means**; wasting time searching for something that isn't there; failing to understand the **whole sentence** (over-focusing on keywords).
**Strategy (short form):**
1. Read instructions (confirm it's TFNG not YNNG).
2. **Read the whole statement** — meaning, not keywords. Note **qualifying words** (some/all, female/male, etc.).
3. Think of **synonyms** likely in the text.
4. **Match the statement to its location** (scan for words/synonyms).
5. **Re-read the statement**, then **close-read** that section.
6. Decide: meaning **matches → True**; **contradicts → False**; **not enough info / not there → Not Given.** Keep it simple.

## 7) YES / NO / NOT GIVEN  → **deep dive in `07`**
Same as TFNG **but about the writer's OPINION/views**, not facts.
**Key problems:** confusing TFNG (fact) with YNNG (opinion); not understanding the **writer's** opinion; wasting time on "not there"; confusing the **writer's** opinion with **other people's** opinions in the text.
**Strategy:** Exactly the same as TFNG, **but focus on the writer's opinion**, not facts. (Yes = agrees with writer; No = contradicts writer; Not Given = no info to judge the writer's view.)

## 8) MATCHING SENTENCE ENDINGS (matching sentences) *(rare, be ready)*
Given sentence **beginnings** + a list of **endings**; match them.
**Key problem:** using your **own logic or grammar** instead of the **text** ("those two go together logically / grammatically").
**Strategy:**
1. **Read the sentence beginnings first**; understand them.
2. **Predict** the endings before looking.
3. **Then** read the endings; **match the obvious ones** (extremely clear ones).
4. **Eliminate obviously wrong** endings; shortlist 1–2 possibles.
5. **Locate** the relevant text; **close-read**; choose. *(Everything based on the TEXT, not logic/grammar.)*

## 9) MATCHING NAMES
List of names ↔ statements (often research findings: scientists/researchers — tends to appear **toward the end**).
**Key problem:** writing names immediately without reading the text in detail (students are tired/rushed at the end).
**Strategy:**
1. **Scan the text for the names; underline** where each appears.
2. **Start with names mentioned only ONCE** (their info is in a small area → find those answers first).
3. **Read that research in detail; match** to the statement.
4. **Delete the statement** you've matched (easier to differentiate the rest).
5. Move to the next single-mention name; then names mentioned twice; repeat until all matched.

## 10) MATCHING INFORMATION ("Which paragraph contains…?")
Match pieces of information to the paragraph that contains them.
**Key problem:** you must consider the **whole text** — huge amount to digest.
**Strategy:**
1. **Do this question LAST** within its section — if it appears first, **skip it**, finish the other questions first (you'll have familiarised yourself with the text → far easier, less info to re-process).
2. **Read the questions first**; think of **synonyms**.
3. **Skim the whole text** to understand it.
4. **Re-read the questions**; think about **location** — some will be obvious.
5. **Scan to find the location; underline.**
6. **Check the statement; mark correct** if you can (easy ones save time).
7. If you can't find it quickly, **move to a different paragraph** — the answer often "presents itself" in the next one. **Have the confidence to move on** (don't burn 5 min on one paragraph).

## 11) TABLE COMPLETION / FLOWCHART COMPLETION
Fill missing info into a table (more common in **Academic**) or flowchart (common in **General Training**). *Same principles.*
**Key problem:** **not reading instructions** (one word / one word + a number, etc.).
**Strategy:**
1. **Read the instructions carefully.**
2. **Scan the text to locate the correct paragraph.**
3. **Read that section carefully** (don't just skim — read it).
4. **Transfer the words EXACTLY** as they appear into the gap.
5. **Check spelling** (you're copying exact letters — "no excuse" to misspell).

## 12) MATCHING HEADINGS  → **deep dive in `08`**
List of headings (always **more headings than paragraphs**) ↔ paragraphs; match each paragraph to a heading.
**Key problems:** lots of info to process (you'll read the whole text); students wrongly read only first/last sentence or focus on keywords; not enough time spent understanding the **differences between headings**; failing to grasp the **general meaning** of each paragraph (the real thing tested).
**Strategy (short form):**
1. **Do this question FIRST** in its section (you read the whole text anyway → makes other questions easier).
2. **Ignore the headings entirely at first.**
3. **Read each paragraph and write YOUR OWN heading** (forces you to understand the general meaning — what's tested).
4. **Then** look at the headings; understand the **differences in meaning**.
5. **Match the obvious ones** (your heading ≈ a listed heading → correct).
6. For the rest, **close-read** the paragraph and choose based on meaning differences. Move on if stuck; come back.

## 🧷 PATTERNS THAT REPEAT ACROSS TYPES
- **Read the questions/statements before the text** (completion, TFNG, MCQ, matching, headings — almost always).
- **"Where before what"** + **synonym awareness** — universal.
- **Predict** before you read (completion, sentence-endings, diagrams).
- **Word count + spelling** — the silent score-killers in all completion/labelling/table types.
- **Confidence to move on** — don't get trapped on one item (matching info, headings, Not Given).
- **Order tricks:** Matching Headings → do **first**; Matching Information → do **last**.

🇧🇩 **বাংলায়:** প্রতিটা question type আলাদা sub-skill টেস্ট করে — তাই আলাদা step-by-step। প্রায় সব ক্ষেত্রে: আগে **প্রশ্ন/statement** পড়ো, **synonym** ভাবো, **"আগে কোথায় (location), পরে কী (answer)"**, তারপর **close reading**। Completion/diagram/table-এ **word limit + বানান** খেয়াল রাখো। **Matching Headings আগে করো, Matching Information শেষে করো।** আটকে গেলে confidence নিয়ে পরেরটায় যাও।
