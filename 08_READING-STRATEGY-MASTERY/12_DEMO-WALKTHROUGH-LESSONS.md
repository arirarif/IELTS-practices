# 12 — LESSONS FROM THE LIVE DEMO TEST (expert walkthrough)

> Chris does a full demo of a real practice test (got Band 9 on the real test "in 20 minutes"). These are the **concrete, reusable lessons** the demo proves — the strategies from the other files **in action**.

## 🔧 THE PRE-FLIGHT MOVE (do this at the start of EVERY section)
- **First, check WHICH question types are in this section.** If a type that needs whole-text understanding (e.g. **Matching Headings**) is present, **do that one first** — even if it's printed later. If none, dive straight in.
- This is the **ordering strategy** applied: Matching Headings **first**; Matching Information **last**.

---

## 🌞 PART 1 DEMO — Labelling a Diagram (layers of the Sun) + Sentence Completion + TFNG

### Labelling a diagram (the Sun's layers)
- **Read instructions:** "no more than two words" → 1 or 2 words, never 3+.
- **Study the diagram first — don't panic** (you don't need to be a physicist). *Predicting wrongly is fine* — Chris guessed "radiation" for #1 and was proven wrong by the text, then corrected. **Studying the diagram first prevents misconceptions.**
- **Where before what:** scanned for "visible surface = Photosphere", then "directly above the Photosphere = Chromosphere", "the outermost layer / atmosphere = Corona", "just outside the core = radiative zone", "surrounding the radiative zone = convection zone" (the up/down arrows in the diagram matched "hot material rises and plunges back down" → confirmation).
- **Lesson:** the arrows/visual details are **clues** that confirm the location. Check the word limit + spelling before moving on.

### Sentence completion (matching sentence endings A–I)
- **Read the incomplete sentence first; predict by GRAMMAR** ("the vast majority of total mass is accounted for by ___" → must be a **noun / thing with mass**).
- **Eliminate by grammar:** several endings don't fit **grammatically** → cross them out, narrowing to the one that fits ("...there is no fixed outer edge ___" → only one option is grammatical = **I**). **This is *why* you predict — grammar reveals the answer even when you don't know the science** (plasma/helium/hydrogen).
- **Synonyms decide it:** "a fraction of the light" (text) = "a small proportion of the light" (option) → direct synonyms. "skin damage / sunburn" = synonyms. **These are vocabulary questions as much as reading questions.**

### TFNG in the demo (worked logic)
- *"interior composed of a mixture of **only** two elements"* → text says **"almost entirely** hydrogen and helium" → "almost entirely" ≠ "only two" → **FALSE** (the qualifying word **only** is the key).
- *"movement between radiative zone & surrounding area gives rise to solar flares"* → text: convection-zone movement "helps create solar flares" → **TRUE.**
- *"most of the surface is not yellow"* → text lists colours (oranges, yellows, reds) but **never gives proportions** → can't confirm "most" → **NOT GIVEN.**
- *"light travels faster through the chromosphere than the radiative zone"* → text gives gas speed in chromosphere and photon time in radiative zone but **never compares the two** → **NOT GIVEN.**
- *"nearly all energy on Earth is produced by a nuclear reaction"* → text: sun's core nuclear **fusion** (a nuclear reaction) provides "almost all" energy → "nearly all" = "almost all" → **TRUE.**
- **Lesson:** decisions hinge on **qualifying words** (only / most / nearly all) and on whether the text **actually compares / quantifies** — if it doesn't, it's **Not Given**, not False.

## 🌊 PART 2 DEMO — Matching Headings + Short Answer + MCQ (Oceania passage)
- **Matching Headings done FIRST.** Wrote own headings per section: "effect of European colonisation" → matched given heading **"effect of human migration"** (Europeans = humans); "impact of invasive species" → **"impact of foreign wildlife"** (near-exact match) → delete used headings → continue. Strategic elimination (do E & F to narrow down a stuck D).
- **Short answer:** *vocabulary is decisive* — if you don't know **"dairy industry"** you'd wrongly grab "New Zealand cats"; the answer "high levels of water pollution" must be trimmed to the **word limit** → **"water pollution"** (2 words). "strict quarantine", "six years" (number acceptable), "90%", "coral bleaching".
- **Lesson:** because Matching Headings forced you to read the whole text first, the short-answer + MCQ become **much faster** — the hard work was already done.
- **MCQ trap (Great Pacific Garbage Patch):** "as big as Texas" appears but text says **"some have theorised"** (a theory ≠ a known fact) → wrong; correct = **"of unknown size"** (small pieces, mostly below surface, hard to estimate). **Lesson: keyword-spotting gives location, NOT the answer — close-read the qualifier ("some theorise" vs "is known to").**

## 🗺️ PART 3 DEMO — Table Completion + Matching Information + Matching Names (Columbus passage)
- **Table completion:** scan for dates/names; mind word limit ("sent to **the king and queen**"); confirm the pronoun "he" really refers to Columbus before writing "arrival in the Bahamas"; "naming of America" (after Amerigo).
- **Matching Information:** "Ferdinand & Isabella first refused to fund Columbus" → "rebuffed" (guess meaning from context = refused); move on if not found and the answer "presents itself."
- **Matching Names:** **start with names mentioned only once** (Francisco Pizarro appears once → quick) to narrow down; use **elimination** for the heavily-mentioned ones (Columbus appears everywhere). "Italian explorer" = Columbus (from Genoa); "finally conquered the Iberian Peninsula" = Ferdinand & Isabella (by elimination + logic: they're the king & queen).

## 🎓 THREE WAYS TO USE A DEMO/PRACTICE TEST (Chris's advice)
1. **Best:** download the test, **do it yourself first without checking answers**, then watch the walkthrough and compare — *same method as the expert = on track; different = change things.*
2. **Relaxing:** just watch and absorb how an expert thinks.
3. **Least useful:** check all answers immediately, then read why they're correct.

## 🪙 THE TWO BIG TAKEAWAYS FROM THE DEMO
1. **Having a strategy + correct ORDER saves huge time** (Matching Headings first → later questions become easy because you already understood the text).
2. **It's reading + vocabulary AND being smart (strategy/order)** — both together.

🇧🇩 **বাংলায়:** ডেমো যা প্রমাণ করে: প্রতিটা section-এ **আগে question type চেক করো** (Matching Headings থাকলে আগে করো)। diagram **আগে বুঝে নাও** (ভয় নয়, ভুল prediction-ও সমস্যা নয়)। completion-এ **grammar দিয়ে predict ও eliminate** করো — বিজ্ঞান না জানলেও grammar উত্তর দেখিয়ে দেয়। **synonym**-ই উত্তর মেলায় (fraction=small proportion, sunburn=skin damage)। TFNG-তে **qualifying words** (only/most/nearly all) আর "text কি আদৌ তুলনা/পরিমাণ দিয়েছে" — না দিলে **Not Given**। MCQ-তে keyword শুধু **জায়গা** দেয়, উত্তর নয় ("some theorise" ≠ "is known to")। Matching Names-এ **একবার উল্লেখিত নাম আগে**, বাকিগুলো elimination-এ।
