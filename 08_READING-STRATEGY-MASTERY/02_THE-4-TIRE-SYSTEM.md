# 02 — THE 4-TIRE SYSTEM (Why your score isn't increasing)

> This is the diagnostic heart of the whole course. Chris's answer to *"Why is my IELTS reading score not increasing no matter how much I work?"*

---

## THE TWO FRUSTRATED STUDENT TYPES (do you recognise yourself?)
When students email "my score isn't improving", it's almost always one of these:
1. *"I've completed a reading course / I'm following all these strategies — and my score isn't going up."*
2. *"I've done ALL the practice tests I can find — all the Cambridge books, all the online tests — and my score is still the same."*

**The hard truth:** Relying on *doing lots of practice tests* will not raise your score **at all**. It can even **lower** it — because if you practise the **wrong things**, you get better at doing them **wrong**. Likewise, learning strategies *wrong* decreases your score.

> 🏎️ **This is literally your situation** — "I'm solving so many passages and it's not helping." This file is the fix.

---

## 🚗 THE CAR WITH 4 TIRES (the central metaphor)
A car can be a brand-new $100,000 Tesla — but if **one tire is busted, the car is completely useless.** The 4 tires are the 4 things below. **Miss even one → it doesn't matter how many tests or courses you do, your score will NOT increase.** And you **must do them in order.**

---

### 🛞 TIRE 1 — A strategy for EACH question type
- Different question types (e.g. True/False/Not Given vs Matching Headings) test **different sub-skills** → each needs a **very different strategy**.
- **Common failure:** student learns a strategy, does a few questions, says *"your strategy doesn't work."* → That's a problem with the **approach**, not the strategy.
- **The goal is not to look at the strategy once — it is to MASTER it.**
- **"Master" defined:** you could **teach it to someone else with zero notes** — step 1, step 2, step 3, step 4. On test day you do it **automatically, without thinking.**
- A strategy you've *looked at* but not mastered = a **broken tire**. Useless.

### 🛞 TIRE 2 — Practice tests (used the RIGHT way, in two goals)
Belief "more practice tests = higher score" is **false**. Approach them differently, in **two goals**:
- **Goal 1 — Master the strategies.** Take a step-by-step strategy + some practice questions and **take as long as you want. Time does NOT matter.** 1.5h, 2h, 3h for one test is *fine*. The mistake students make: doing a test under exam conditions, getting a low score, then blaming the strategy or themselves ("I'm not good enough, I'll fail, I hate this test") and giving up. **Don't.**
- **Goal 2 — Find your weakness.** Once comfortable with the strategies, **now** do tests **under exam conditions** — purpose: to surface your weaknesses (→ Tire 3).

### 🛞 TIRE 3 — Weakness analysis (study your mistakes)
- **Think deeply about each mistake** to find **patterns** — the common reasons you score low.
- Example: 10 wrong, **8 are spelling** → spelling is the pattern.
- Possible root causes: confused about a question-type strategy / vocabulary gap (didn't understand words) / **scanning** issue (can't find the location) / **skimming** issue (can't get general meaning) / a **specific question type** you always fail (e.g. every TFNG or every MCQ).
- Skip this and you have a **busted tire** — nothing else matters.
- *(Full method + the 5 mistake categories + student case-studies → `04_WEAKNESS-ANALYSIS-DIAGNOSTIC.md`.)*

### 🛞 TIRE 4 — Work on your weaknesses (turn weakness → strength)
- Knowing your weakness is **not enough**. Chris has 1-on-1 students who were told *exactly* the one reason (e.g. "vocabulary + this question type") — then did **nothing**, took the test, and **failed.**
- Take the weak area and **convert it into a strength** (improve vocabulary; master that question type).
- **🎯 Sniper, not shotgun:** don't try to improve *everything* — focus on **one or two** weaknesses.

---

## ⏳ THE 5TH PART — TIME (the binding rule)
Students say: *"I don't have time to do all this — Chris, just give me quick tips!"* → **No. There are no quick tips that increase your score.**

- **Pharmacist analogy:** You come in with a headache, she gives paracetamol, you return 5 minutes later angry it "didn't work." Unreasonable — **certain things take time.**
- **Two-students analogy:** Both start June 1st.
  - Student A books the test for July 1st and rushes everything into 30 days. → **Probably fails.**
  - Student B ignores the booking date and just focuses on **the process**: step 1 → 2 → 3 → 4. → **Gets the score in the *quickest possible* time.**
- **Flat-tire analogy:** Changing a tire properly takes ~30 minutes. Demand it in 5 minutes and the car goes **nowhere** and you're just frustrated. Students who fail repeatedly are rushing instead of doing it right.

---

## ✅ RECAP (say this back to yourself)
1. Get strategies that **actually work** → **master** them (teach-without-notes level) → use them automatically on test day.
2. Do a **weakness analysis** on your practice tests.
3. **Work on those weaknesses** — turn them into strengths.
4. Respect **time** — do it right, not fast.

> Miss one step (don't master strategies / don't find weaknesses / don't fix weaknesses) → **you will fail.** Do all four in order → **great results.**
