# 03 — THE ULTIMATE BAND 9 WORKFLOW (the 5-step loop)

> *"The most powerful method for improving your reading is understanding your own personal weaknesses and then improving those weaknesses."*
> This is the exact loop Chris says has produced **more Band 9 students than any other** strategy. It is the same idea as the 4-Tire system, expressed as a repeatable **5-step procedure** you run on every real practice test.

---

## 🩺 THE GUIDING ANALOGIES
- **Doctor analogy:** A doctor doesn't keep running tests until you're better — they **diagnose**, then **treat the problem.** Band 9 students do practice tests to **diagnose weaknesses**, then fix them *outside* of practice.
- **Gym analogy:** Your muscles don't grow *in* the gym — they grow **after** you leave. Improvement comes **after** practice, from what you do with the results. (Over-train / over-practice with bad habits → you get *smaller*, i.e. worse.)
- **Farm-driver analogy:** A friend drove a car around a farm daily for **7 years** and still **failed his driving test repeatedly**, while friends with **6 weeks** of proper practice passed first time. Why? **Bad habits, never corrected.** Mindless practice **entrenches** bad habits. (This is exactly the "doing tons of tests but not improving" trap.)

---

## 🔁 THE 5 STEPS

### STEP 1 — Get REAL practice tests (never fake)
- Only reliable sources: **Cambridge.org, ielts.org, IDP, British Council, and the Cambridge books** (Cambridge IELTS series).
- Get **two or three** tests you have **never seen before.**
- The Cambridge books cost ~$20–30 but are effectively "free" vs the ~$200–300 wasted on a failed test. **Don't download fake free PDFs** — most online PDFs are fake/misleading and cause failure.

### STEP 2 — Do them UNDER EXAM CONDITIONS
- **Don't cheat:** a test you've never seen; don't peek at answers first.
- **Quiet room, no distractions, exact official timing (60 minutes).** Don't go even 30 seconds over.

### STEP 3 — HONEST correction
- Correct exactly like the real test. **One letter wrong = wrong.** Kind-of-the-same = wrong. Don't gift yourself marks ("I'll do better on the day"). You're only cheating yourself — and the strategy won't work.

### STEP 4 — Ask WHY you got each one wrong (the most important step — look for PATTERNS)
- Put ✗ beside wrong, ✓ beside right. For **every** ✗, think **deeply** about *why*. **Write the reason down** in a book/on paper — multiple pages if needed — so patterns emerge.
- This may be quick (a spelling slip) or slow (vocabulary/meaning/synonyms). **It is an investment of time, not wasted time.**
- Typical patterns: ~80% spelling → spelling is the weakness · couldn't find answer because words unknown → vocabulary · always wrong on a question type (e.g. TFNG) → strategy issue with that type · fine in Sections 1–2 but most wrong in Section 3 → reading skills/vocabulary · ran out of time near 60 min → skills/vocabulary but mainly **strategy/efficiency**.

### STEP 5 — WORK ON THE WEAKNESS (turn it into a strength)
- Hardest but most important step. Spelling problem → improve spelling; vocabulary → improve vocabulary; strategy → learn/master the strategy.
- **Don't just cry about it** ("I'm terrible at spelling"). The difference between people who succeed and fail: the successful feel uncomfortable but **keep working** and convert weaknesses to strengths.
- **Tackle your BIGGEST weakness first** — it's hard, but clearing it makes everything else far easier (an 80/20 effect: fixing the top weakness can eliminate 80–90% of mistakes).

> Then **loop:** do another real test → diagnose new weaknesses → fix → repeat. You only re-do tests to **check if you improved** and **find new weaknesses.**

---

## 📏 THE READINESS RULE (when are you allowed to book the test?)
- **Rule:** When you score **half a band above your target**, achieved **three times in a row**, you're ready.
- Example: target **Band 8** → you must hit **8.5+ three times running.**
- If you need 8 but get 7.5 / 8 / 7.5 → you'll most likely get **7–7.5 on the real day** (stress lowers performance; the real test is not harder, just more stressful).
- **#1 reason students fail (>99% of the time):** they **book the test before they're ready.** Consistently hit your needed score *first*, and you're "pretty much guaranteed" the score on the day.
