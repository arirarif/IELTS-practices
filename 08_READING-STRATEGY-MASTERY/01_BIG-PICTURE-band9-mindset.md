# 01 — THE BIG PICTURE: Band 9 Mindset & Format Facts

> Everything in this file is what Chris says **Band 9 students understand** that band 5/6/7 students don't. These are not tips — they are the *worldview* you must adopt first.

---

## A. FORMAT FACTS YOU MUST INTERNALISE

1. **Every reading test looks the same structurally:** 3 sections (Section 1, 2, 3) and roughly **10–11 (up to 12) different question types**. The *combination* of question types varies test to test, but the structure (3 sections) never changes.

2. **It gets progressively harder:** Section 1 = easiest, Section 2 = harder, Section 3 = hardest. **Why?** Section 3 is what separates band 7 from band 8 from band 9. The hard questions are placed at the end so a candidate who is "really" band 8 will drop the last couple and land at their true level.

3. **Academic vs General Training = "same but different":**
   - **Different:** Academic uses academic/university-textbook texts; GT uses everyday texts.
   - **Same:** the **question types** and the **strategies** are *identical*. Band 9 students practise with **both** academic and GT papers because the skills transfer.
   - **Same difficulty (myth-buster):** GT needs **40/40** for Band 9; Academic lets you get **39/40 (one wrong)** for Band 9. So GT is *not* "much easier" — that belief comes from Academic candidates who failed and want to feel better.

4. **Computer-based vs paper-based:** No score difference — they test your ability to **read in English**, not on a screen vs paper. BUT computer-based is **less stressful & more convenient** (e.g. just click the MCQ option, no transferring answers). **If you have the choice, choose computer-based** — it won't raise your score but it reduces stress.

5. **The test is a VOCABULARY test as much as a reading test.** Questions use **synonyms** of the words in the text. Wide vocabulary is what lets you locate answers and decide between options. Many "reading skill" problems are really vocabulary problems in disguise.

---

## B. THE CORE BELIEFS OF A BAND 9 STUDENT

1. **A separate strategy for each question type.** Each question type tests **different reading sub-skills**, so each needs a **unique strategy**. Band 5/6/7 students use *one* approach for everything — Chris has *never* met a Band 9 student who did that.

2. **Master the strategy — don't just look at it once.** "Master" = you could **teach it to someone else with no notes.** On test day you apply it **automatically, without thinking** — like tying your shoelaces or riding a bike.

3. **It is extremely rare to just be "bad at reading."** There is *always* a specific reason for a low score. Find the reason.

4. **You will NOT understand every word — and that's fine.** Even Oxford/Cambridge professors wouldn't know every word in some passages. Your job is **not** to understand every word; your job is to **get as many correct answers as possible.**

5. **Don't panic when you don't know a word.** Mindset is the biggest difference between band 7 and band 9 here. Band 9 reaction: *"Do I need to know this word? No → move on."*

6. **Strategies are a guide, not magic** (the Sherpa analogy): Even the world's best mountain climber hires a Sherpa to guide the safe route. Strategies guide you — but if your underlying reading/vocabulary is weak (you "can't get to base camp"), the best guide can't save you. **Strategies + real skill, together.**

7. **Tips and tricks do NOT work.** They're attractive because they're easy, but they don't make you a better reader. There is no "one magic strategy for every question."

---

## C. THE 3 TIPS-AND-TRICKS BAND 9 STUDENTS ACTUALLY USE (the real ones)

> From the "Ultimate Guide" lesson — these are habits, not gimmicks.

- **Tip 1 — Treat reading as a vocabulary test.** When reading anything, see a word you don't know → write it in a vocabulary notebook, look up the meaning, add synonyms. You improve reading *and* vocabulary simultaneously.
- **Tip 2 — Accept you won't know every word.** Deploy systems to cope (see Tip below).
- **Tip 3 — Read ACTIVELY, not passively.** (Full detail in `05_CORE-READING-SKILLS.md`.)
- **Guess meaning from context:** when you hit an unknown word, *guess* its meaning from surrounding words, then look it up — 95%+ of the time you'll be right. This is how native speakers read.
- **Read the instructions carefully** — Band 9 students are calmer and actually do this (word limits etc.).
- **Be very good at spelling** — one letter wrong = whole answer wrong. Band 9 students don't complain; they fix it via active reading.
- **Practise slow AND fast** (see `10_PRACTICE-PRINCIPLES.md`).

---

## D. MINDSET RULES (the attitude that wins)

- Don't cry/complain about your weakness ("I'm terrible at spelling"). Everyone is bad at *something*. Successful people feel uncomfortable but **keep working** and convert the weakness to a strength.
- **"You're not bad at it — you're not good at it *yet*."** (Said to a student who hated TFNG and matching headings; he went from ~6 to ~7.5 after focusing on them.)
- **Success leaves clues:** when you see successful (Band 9) people all doing the *same* thing, copy them.
- **Don't improve by watching YouTube** — improve by **taking action**. Most people watch one more video and never act.

🇧🇩 **বাংলায় মূল কথা:** প্রতিটা question type আলাদা — তাই আলাদা strategy লাগবে, আর সেটা এমনভাবে রপ্ত করতে হবে যেন পরীক্ষায় চিন্তা না করেই আপনাআপনি প্রয়োগ হয়। সব শব্দ বোঝা লাগবে না; কাজ হলো সর্বোচ্চ সঠিক উত্তর পাওয়া। "Trick" বলে কিছু নেই — strategy + আসল reading skill + vocabulary, একসাথে।
