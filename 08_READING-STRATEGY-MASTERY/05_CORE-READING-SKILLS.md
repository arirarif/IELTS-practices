# 05 — CORE READING SKILLS (the tools every strategy uses)

> Strategies just tell you **which skill to use, when, and in what order.** These are the underlying skills. What Band 9 students understand that others don't: **when to use each skill, when NOT to, and in what order.**

---

## 🛠️ THE 3 CORE SKILLS

### 1. SKIMMING — read fast for GENERAL meaning
- Everyday example: reading a **newspaper / news website** — you don't read every word, you skim to get the gist.
- In the test: read a paragraph **quickly** to understand **generally what it's about**.
- The key skill for **Matching Headings** (you can't read every word — get the general meaning of each paragraph).

### 2. SCANNING — search for SPECIFIC information / location
- Everyday example: looking at **cinema/movie times** — you don't read everything, you scan for the movie name + a convenient time.
- In the test: scan for **keywords from the question or their synonyms** — **NOT to find the answer, but to find the LOCATION** of the answer.
- The key sub-skill behind **Matching** question types.

### 3. CLOSE READING — read carefully to DECIDE the answer (most important & most overlooked)
- Reading **carefully around the location** you identified, **plus** re-reading the question carefully, then **deciding** the correct answer.
- **This is what Band 9 students spend MORE of their time on** than band 6/7 students.
- Skimming & scanning are the **opposite** of close reading — they're just **tools to find the location**; close reading **finds the answer.**

---

## 🧭 THE GOLDEN PRINCIPLE: "WHERE BEFORE WHAT"
> During the test, **first find WHERE** the answer is (the location) — **before** you decide **WHAT** the answer is.
1. Scan for the **location** (don't decide the answer yet).
2. **Then** close-read that section to decide the answer.

This saves huge time: instead of reading 6–7 paragraphs, you focus on the **one** paragraph that holds the answer. Strategic with **thought, intention, and time.**

> Different strategies order the skills differently: some skim first then scan; some skip skimming and scan first; some focus on understanding the *questions* first, others on the *text* first. **There is no single universal order** — it depends on the question type.

---

## 📖 ACTIVE READING vs PASSIVE READING (the habit that builds everything)
- **Passive reading** = reading just for enjoyment / "reading's sake" → leads to **near-zero improvement** for IELTS.
- **Active reading** = reading **while working on something** at the same time: improving reading skill, vocabulary, grammar, or spelling.
- Examples of active reading:
  - Hit an unknown word → **guess meaning from context**, *then* look it up (don't just move on).
  - Write the new word in a **vocabulary notebook** with meaning + synonyms.
  - Notice **punctuation/grammar** ("why is there a comma here, not there?") if grammar is your weakness.
  - See a weirdly-spelled word → write the **correct spelling** down, if spelling is your weakness.
- **Power line:** *"10 minutes of active reading will improve you more than 1 hour of passive reading."*
- **You MUST read to get Band 9** — "anyone who thinks they'll get Band 9 in reading without reading is insane." Read things you **enjoy** (Band 9 interviewees read BBC articles, wildlife topics, etc.) so it's **sustainable and regular**, and read **actively**.

---

## 🔤 VOCABULARY — the hidden score driver
- **The reading test is a vocabulary test as much as a reading test.** Synonyms in the questions vs text are *how* you locate answers.
- A "time management problem" is **often actually a vocabulary problem** — you can't find the words fast enough.
- A low score is often **not** a reading-skill issue — it's that you don't know the words / the differences between words.
- **Best way to build vocabulary = reading** (active reading + notebook). No magic word-list; the *range* of vocabulary is the big Band 7 → Band 9 difference.

---

## 🧩 COPING WITH UNKNOWN WORDS (Band 9 systems)
1. **Guess meaning from context** — look at surrounding words; you'll be right 95%+ of the time. Native speakers do exactly this (they don't constantly check dictionaries).
2. **Don't panic** — ask *"Do I need this word to find the answer?"* Often **no** → move on. Your job is correct answers, not understanding every word.
3. After guessing, **look it up** to confirm and learn it (active reading).

🇧🇩 **বাংলায়:** ৩টা মূল skill — **Skimming** (দ্রুত পড়ে general meaning), **Scanning** (keyword/synonym দিয়ে উত্তরের **জায়গা** খোঁজা, উত্তর নয়), **Close reading** (ওই জায়গা মন দিয়ে পড়ে উত্তর ঠিক করা — সবচেয়ে গুরুত্বপূর্ণ, সবচেয়ে অবহেলিত)। সোনালী নিয়ম: **"Where before what"** — আগে কোথায়, পরে কী। **Active reading** করো (পড়ার সময় শব্দ/grammar/বানান শেখো) — ১০ মিনিট active reading = ১ ঘণ্টা passive reading-এর চেয়ে ভালো। Vocabulary-ই বড় পার্থক্য; অজানা শব্দে ভয় না পেয়ে context থেকে আন্দাজ করো।
