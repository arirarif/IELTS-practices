# 09 — THE TIME-MANAGEMENT TRUTH

> The most counter-intuitive lesson in the course: **time-management "tips" do not exist.** What Band 9 students do about time is completely different from what everyone else asks for.

---

## 🏃 THE CORE PRINCIPLE: "You don't need time-management tips — you need to get better at reading."
**The Usain Bolt analogy (repeated several times):**
- The runners finishing 2nd/3rd/4th behind Usain Bolt don't lose because of bad *time management* — they lose because **Bolt is simply better at running.**
- Bolt didn't Google "top 10 magic time-management tips" the night before the Olympic final — he **spent years becoming better at running.**
- **Same with IELTS:** you finish on time because you're **better at reading** (skills + vocabulary + spelling + strategy + practice), **not** because of time tricks.

> Chris's own example: he got Band 9 on the real test **in 20 minutes**, then "went to the toilet and stared at the ceiling" — not magic, not tips: he's a native speaker + IELTS teacher who is **very good at reading.** (He was "in the pub the night before," to make the point that no time-tips were involved.)

---

## ⚙️ HOW SPEED ACTUALLY COMES: master strategies until AUTOMATIC
- Band 9 students **memorise a separate strategy per question type** "by heart." Put a TFNG or MCQ in front of them and they **don't think about the strategy** — they just do it.
- Analogy: **riding a bike / tying shoelaces** — done so many times it's natural. That automaticity is what makes them **complete everything very quickly.**
- This **reduces cognitive load & stress** → leaves more energy for the other 3 skills on test day.

---

## ⏱️ THE ONE REAL "TIME" HABIT BAND 9 STUDENTS DO (section pacing)
The three sections get **progressively harder** (1 easy → 2 medium → 3 hardest). So:
- **Band 9 students do NOT spend equal time on each question/section.** Equal time is a **bad system** — you over-spend on easy questions and starve the hard ones.
- They **spend very little time on Section 1**, finishing it **correctly but as fast as possible**, to bank **more time for Sections 2 & 3** (the difficult end questions where deep thinking is needed).
- **Real Band 9 methods from interviewees:**
  - **Shrubani:** did **time-bound practice**, tracked each section's time in an **Excel sheet**; noticed she over-spent on Sections 1–2 (e.g. 10 min) → trained it down to 7, then targeted **5 minutes** → finished within time.
  - **Jaswant:** aims to finish **Sections 1 & 2 in ~30 minutes** → leaves "plenty of time" for Section 3.

> So: **don't allocate fixed minutes per question.** Get the easy early sections done fast & accurately; reserve thinking time for the hard end.

---

## 🐢→🐇 SLOW-THEN-FAST (how to build speed without panic)
- Beginners (esp. learning new strategies / working on weaknesses) should practise **extremely slowly** — even **30–60 minutes for 6 TFNG questions** — because the goal is **perfecting the strategy**, not the correct answer or speed.
- Then build speed **bit by bit:** real students went 3–4 hours → 2.5h → 2h → 1.5h → 1h15 → 1h05 → eventually **60 minutes.**
- **Driving analogy:** on lesson 1 the instructor doesn't take you to the motorway at 100 mph — you'd crash. Band 6/7 students "crash" by forcing everything into 60 minutes too early, then beat themselves up. **You're not bad at reading — you just aren't ready for full speed yet.**

---

## 🚫 What time management is NOT (and the trap)
- **Not** a set of tips/tricks/hacks looked up the night before.
- A perceived "time problem" is **often actually a VOCABULARY problem** (you can't find words fast enough) — fix vocabulary, the speed follows.
- For a true timing issue: familiarise with all question types → learn their strategies → then **slowly build up speed** (90 min → 80 → 70 → 60).
- **Priyanka** "never faced a time-management problem" — because her **reading level** was high. The lesson isn't "you can't learn from her" — it's that **improving reading removes timing problems.**

🇧🇩 **বাংলায়:** Time-management "tips" বলে কিছু নেই — Usain Bolt-এর মতো, তুমি সময়ে শেষ করো কারণ তুমি **reading-এ ভালো**, কোনো ট্রিকের জন্য নয়। strategy এতবার অনুশীলন করো যেন **চিন্তা ছাড়াই** প্রয়োগ হয় (সাইকেল চালানোর মতো) — এতে দ্রুত হবে, stress কমবে। আসল time-habit: তিন section ক্রমশ কঠিন, তাই **Section 1-এ কম সময়** (দ্রুত+নির্ভুল), কঠিন Section 3-এর জন্য সময় জমাও (Jaswant: ১ ও ২ → ৩০ মিনিটে)। শুরুতে **খুব ধীরে** অনুশীলন (৬ প্রশ্নে ৩০–৬০ মিনিটও ঠিক আছে), পরে ধীরে ধীরে গতি বাড়াও।
