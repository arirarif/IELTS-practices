# 🎧 02 — LISTENING PLAYBOOK

> **Target: Band 7.5 (~32/40).** This is a *banking* module — drillable to near-certainty. Most lost marks here are careless, not "I didn't understand."

---

## 🟦 GOLDEN RULES (read before every session)

1. ✍️ **Spelling & plurals are marked wrong if wrong.** You heard it right but spelled "accommodation" with one *m*? Zero marks. → Fixing spelling alone can gain **2–3 marks** with no extra listening skill.
2. 🎣 **Watch for distractors** — the speaker says something, then **corrects it.** Trigger words: *but, however, actually, no wait, sorry, I mean, originally… eventually.* The answer is almost always the **second** (corrected) info.
3. 👀 **Read questions BEFORE the audio plays.** Use every gap of silence to read ahead. Predict the *type* of answer (number? name? place? noun?).
4. 🔢 **Numbers, dates, times** are Section 1 trap-central. Write them exactly as heard.
5. ⏩ **Never stop to dwell.** Miss one? Let it go instantly — the audio doesn't wait, and chasing it costs you the next 3 answers.

---

## 🗂️ The 4 Sections (what to expect)

| Section | Context | Difficulty | Common traps |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Everyday conversation (e.g. booking, enquiry) | 🟢 Easiest | Numbers, names (spelled out), dates, self-corrections |
| **2** | Monologue, social context (e.g. tour, facility info) | 🟢🟠 | Maps/plans, facility changes ("we moved it to…") |
| **3** | Academic discussion (2–4 speakers) | 🟠🔴 | Opinions evolving, agreement/disagreement, who-said-what |
| **4** | Academic lecture (monologue) | 🔴 Hardest | Dense info, no second chance, paraphrased answers |

---

## 🎯 Strategy by Question Type

### 📝 Form / Note / Sentence Completion
- Check the **word limit** ("NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS"). Three words = wrong, even if correct.
- Predict the answer type from grammar around the gap.
- Answers come **in order.** If you're hunting answer 5 and hear something for 6, you missed 5 — move on.

### 🗺️ Map / Plan Labelling
- **Find your starting point first.** "Turn left" is meaningless if you don't know where you're standing.
- Track direction words: *north/south, left/right, past, opposite, next to, behind.*
- 🪤 Classic trap: *"We were going to put the gym next to the pool, but eventually placed it behind the changing rooms."* → Answer is **behind the changing rooms**, not next to the pool.

### 🔘 Multiple Choice
- The **first plausible-sounding option is usually the distractor.** The answer matches the speaker's **final** choice or a stated restriction.
- Cross out options as they're rejected.

### 🧩 Matching / Categorizing
- Underline the options before audio. These move fast — keep your eyes scanning.

---

## 🏋️ Daily Drills (Phase 1–2)

- **Dictation (10 min):** Play 30 sec, write down every word. Builds spelling + sound-to-word speed.
- **Spelling hit-list:** Keep a list of words you misspell (accommodation, government, restaurant, Wednesday, February…). Drill them weekly.
- **Number/date drills:** Have audio read numbers fast, transcribe them.
- **Section 4 stretch:** Do one Section 4 every other day — it's the hardest, so over-train it.

---

## 📈 How We Hit 7.5

| Week phase | Listening focus |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1–3 | Learn all question types. **Eliminate spelling/number/plural errors** entirely. |
| Weeks 4–7 | Drill distractor recognition. Daily section practice + log *which trap* caught you. |
| Weeks 8–10 | Full timed listening tests, 2×/week. Aim consistent 32+/40. |

> 🔴 **Examiner-strict note:** When you log a listening mock in `07-PROGRESS-TRACKER.md`, don't just write the score — write **which type** each wrong answer was (distractor? spelling? word limit? lost focus?). The *pattern* is the gold.
