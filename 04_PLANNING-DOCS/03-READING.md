# 📖 03 — READING PLAYBOOK (the hard one)

> **Target: Band 7.5 (~30/40 Academic).** Academic Reading is where most people *run out of time*, not out of ability. The whole game is **smart time management + reading for meaning, not keyword-matching.**

---

## 🟥 GOLDEN RULES (read before every session)

1. ⏱️ **20 minutes per passage. Hard cap.** 3 passages, 60 min total. The trap: spending 25 min on the "easy" Passage 1 and reaching Passage 3 with 12 min and 14 blank answers.
2. 🚫 **Do NOT read the whole passage first.** You don't have time. **Skim for structure (1 min), then go question-by-question, scanning** for the relevant spot.
3. 🧠 **Match by MEANING, not matching words.** IELTS deliberately plants questions whose words appear in the passage but mean something different. Exact word-match is often a **trap**.
4. ✍️ **Answers are paraphrased.** The passage rarely uses the same words as the question. Hunt the *idea*, not the vocabulary.
5. ❓ **When stuck >90 sec, guess and move on.** No penalty for wrong answers. A blank = guaranteed zero. **Never leave anything blank.**

---

## ⏱️ The Time Plan (engrave this)

| Stage | Time | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Preview questions | ~60 sec | Scan ALL question types for the passage. Note what each needs (names/dates? paragraph themes? statements to verify?). |
| Skim passage | ~2 min | Get the gist + paragraph topics. Don't read deeply. |
| Answer | ~16 min | Question by question, scan-locate-verify. |
| **Per passage** | **~20 min** | Move on at 20 even if not perfect. |
| Transfer/check | last min | Computer-delivered = no transfer, but review flagged answers. |

> 💡 Passages get **harder 1 → 3.** Some people do Passage 1 fast, then give Passage 3 a few extra minutes. Find your rhythm in practice.

---

## 🎯 Strategy by Question Type (the tricky ones)

### ⚖️ True / False / Not Given  — *the #1 band-killer*
> The single most common mistake: marking **FALSE** when it's actually **NOT GIVEN.**

| Answer | Means | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **TRUE** | Passage *confirms* the statement | The text actively agrees |
| **FALSE** | Passage *contradicts* the statement | The text actively says the **opposite** |
| **NOT GIVEN** | Passage doesn't say either way | The info simply **isn't there** — don't infer, don't use outside knowledge |

- Statements follow **passage order.**
- 🪤 Watch qualifiers: *always, all, some, often, never, only.* They flip meaning. "Scientists always agree" vs "scientists generally agree" → could be FALSE.
- If you're using logic/assumption to decide → it's probably **NOT GIVEN.**

### 🏷️ Matching Headings — *the #2 time-sink*
- **Don't memorize the heading list first** (overloads memory). Instead: **read one paragraph → write your own 3-word summary → then find the closest heading.**
- Match the paragraph's **main point**, not a word that happens to appear in a heading (that's a planted distractor).
- Cross off headings as you use them.

### 🔍 Matching Information / Features / Names
- These do **not** follow passage order — they're scan-heavy and slow. Often best done **last.**
- Scan for capital letters (names), then read around them.

### 📝 Sentence / Summary / Note Completion
- Obey the **word limit.**
- Answer must fit **grammatically** — predict the part of speech needed.
- Words come straight from the passage (completion) — spelling must be exact.

### 🔘 Multiple Choice
- Read the question stem first, then hunt the location, *then* read options. Options are designed to tempt — eliminate aggressively.

## 🏋️ Daily Drills (Phase 1–2)

- **Skimming drill:** Read a passage in 2 min, then write its 1-sentence summary + each paragraph's topic. Builds the "structure-map" reflex.
- **Scanning drill:** Given a name/date/keyword, find it in the passage in <15 sec.
- **T/F/NG isolation drills:** Do sets of *only* T/F/NG until the FALSE-vs-NOT-GIVEN line is instinct.
- **Paraphrase log:** When you find an answer, note how the passage *reworded* the question. Build a feel for IELTS paraphrasing.
- **Vocabulary:** Academic passages reuse academic vocab. Keep a running word list from every passage.

---

## 📈 How We Hit 7.5

| Week phase | Reading focus |
|---|---|
| Weeks 1–3 | Learn every question type. Build skim/scan reflex. **Stop reading the whole passage.** |
| Weeks 4–7 | Hammer T/F/NG and Matching Headings. **Enforce the 20-min-per-passage cap on every practice.** |
| Weeks 8–10 | Full timed reading tests 2×/week. Target consistent 30+/40 *within 60 min*. |

> 🔴 **Examiner-strict note:** Log every wrong answer's **type AND reason** in the tracker — "T/F/NG: marked FALSE, was NOT GIVEN" or "ran out of time on Passage 3." If most errors are timing, the fix is pacing, not vocabulary. If most are T/F/NG, we drill that. **Diagnosis drives the plan.**
