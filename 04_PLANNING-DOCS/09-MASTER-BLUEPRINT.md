# 🎓 09 — MASTER BLUEPRINT (8-Week Intensive · Examiner's Edition)

> Written in the voice of a Senior IELTS Examiner. No fluff. Academic · Computer-delivered · Target **Overall 7.0** (L7.5 / R7.5 / W6.5 / S6.5).
> Assumes ~2.5 hrs/weekday + 4–5 hrs/weekend. The 8-week core fits inside your booked runway; the remaining days become simulation + taper.
> ⚠️ Weak-point targeting is provisional until your **Day-2 diagnostic** — after that, the weekly emphasis self-corrects.

---

# SECTION 1 — THE 8-WEEK STUDY ROADMAP

### 🗓️ WEEK 1 — Diagnosis & Format Mastery
- **Core focus:** Sit ONE full timed test (all modules). Learn the *architecture* of the exam: every section, every question type, the on-screen interface, the clock.
- **Daily split (2.5h):** 60m diagnostic review · 45m read the format/playbook for one module · 30m vocab+grammar habit · 15m speaking aloud.
- **Milestone:** A brutally honest baseline band for each module + a written list of your 3 biggest leaks.
- **🔑 Examiner trick — the Question-Type Audit:** Don't grade by module, grade by *question type*. Tally where you bled marks (e.g. "lost 5 of 6 on T/F/NG"). Bands are won by fixing 2–3 leaky question types, not by "studying more."

### 🗓️ WEEK 2 — Reading T/F/NG + Listening S1–2 + Writing T1 + Speaking P1
- **Core focus:** Reading: True/False/Not Given + Matching Headings. Listening: Sections 1–2, spelling & distractors. Writing: Task 1 structure (intro→overview→2 bodies). Speaking: Part 1 fluency.
- **Daily split (2.5h):** 50m Reading drills · 40m Listening · 40m Writing (1 task) · 20m vocab/grammar. Speaking woven in (talk aloud 15m).
- **Milestone:** Score 9/13 on an isolated T/F/NG set; write one Task 1 with a clear data-free overview; zero spelling errors on a Listening section.
- **🔑 Examiner trick — the NOT GIVEN default:** If you're using *your own logic or outside knowledge* to justify an answer, it's almost always **NOT GIVEN**. FALSE requires the text to actively state the *opposite*. Most Band-6 candidates over-pick FALSE.

### 🗓️ WEEK 3 — Reading detail + Listening S3–4 + Writing T2 structure + Speaking P2
- **Core focus:** Reading: Matching Information/Features, summary completion. Listening: Sections 3–4 (MCQ, maps, lecture). Writing: Task 2 — the 4-paragraph backbone + opinion essays. Speaking: Part 2 cue-card method.
- **Daily split:** 45m Reading · 40m Listening · 50m Writing T2 (+rewrite) · 20m vocab/grammar · 15m cue-card recording.
- **Milestone:** Produce one full Task 2 (250+ words) with a clear position and PEEL bodies; speak a full 2 minutes on a cue card without drying up.
- **🔑 Examiner trick — the 30-second Position Lock:** Before writing a single Task 2 sentence, write your one-line opinion at the top of the page. Every paragraph must visibly serve it. Essays that "discuss" without a locked position cap at Band 6 for Task Response.

### 🗓️ WEEK 4 — Integration + Mid-Point Mock
- **Core focus:** All Task 2 prompt types (opinion, discussion, problem/solution, two-part). Reading under the 20-min/passage guillotine. Listening full 40-Q sets. Speaking Part 3 depth.
- **Daily split:** rotate modules; one weekend **full timed mock**.
- **Milestone:** A full mock that shows movement from the Week-1 baseline. Re-run the Question-Type Audit — the leaks should be shrinking.
- **🔑 Examiner trick — Prediction before audio:** In Listening, before each recording, predict the *part of speech and answer type* for every gap (number? name? plural noun?). You'll catch the answer because you already know its shape.

### 🗓️ WEEK 5 — Weakness Blitz + Language Upgrade
- **Core focus:** Hammer whatever the Week-4 mock exposed. Parallel track: lift Writing & Speaking *language* — coherence, paraphrase, complex sentences.
- **Daily split:** 60m weak-module drilling · 40m Writing depth (rewrite old essays at higher band) · 30m vocab/grammar · 20m speaking fluency.
- **Milestone:** Your weakest module up half a band vs Week 4; 10 new topic collocations actively used in writing/speaking.
- **🔑 Examiner trick — the Paraphrase Ladder & template ban:** Examiners are trained to spot memorised templates ("In this modern era of globalization…") and penalise them under Task Response & Lexical. Instead, paraphrase the *actual* prompt nouns/verbs. Range from precise paraphrase, not recycled phrases.

### 🗓️ WEEK 6 — Full Mocks Begin (×2) + Error Surgery
- **Core focus:** Two full timed mocks under computer-delivered conditions. Deep error analysis after each. Writing 6.5→7 polish.
- **Daily split:** mock days (full) + non-mock days = targeted error review + 1 marked essay.
- **Milestone:** Listening & Reading consistently 30+/40; Writing reliably at 6.5 with the criteria understood.
- **🔑 Examiner trick — the 20-Minute Guillotine:** In Reading, when 20 minutes are up on a passage, you *move on* — full stop. Top scorers aren't faster readers; they refuse to let an "interesting" Passage 1 steal Passage 3's marks.

### 🗓️ WEEK 7 — Peak Simulation
- **Core focus:** Two more full mocks at *exact* exam timing and environment. Lock templates and vocab. Speaking: full mock interviews.
- **Daily split:** mock + review rhythm; eliminate your top-3 recurring errors permanently.
- **Milestone:** You can hit the target distribution under pressure, calmly. Speaking flows for the full Part 2 with extension.
- **🔑 Examiner trick — the Task 1 Overview formula:** Overview = the two *extremes* (highest/lowest) + the overall *direction/trend*, with **no specific data**. Start it with "Overall,". A missing or data-stuffed overview is the single biggest thing keeping Task 1 at Band 6.

### 🗓️ WEEK 8 — Taper & Perform
- **Core focus:** One light mock early. Then review only — model answers, your error log, your vocab bank. **No new material.**
- **Daily split:** short, calm sessions. Last 2 days: rest, sleep, light reading aloud.
- **Milestone:** You walk in having already "taken the test" 6+ times.
- **🔑 Examiner trick — sleep beats cramming:** A rested brain reads faster, hears distractors, and writes cleaner grammar. The night before is for sleep, not new essays. On test day: read every instruction's *word limit*, and never leave a Reading/Listening answer blank.

# SECTION 2 — RESOURCE CURATION & DIET

### 1. Official materials (prioritise these)
- **Cambridge IELTS 17, 18, 19** — retired real papers. Your entire mock diet. Start newest. *(19 → 18 → 17.)*
- **ielts.org → free sample questions** — official format + answer keys.
- **Cambridge Official Guide to IELTS** — for the band descriptors and worked examples.

### 2. Free high-quality repositories
- **British Council "Road to IELTS" (free)** — *computer-delivered* practice; matches your test interface. **Use this for screen practice.**
- **IDP IELTS free practice** — extra full tests.
- **IELTS Liz** (ieltsliz.com / YouTube) — best free Writing & Speaking model answers + tips.
- **IELTS Simon** (ielts-simon.com) — ex-examiner; the gold standard for Writing Task 1 & 2 method.
- **IELTS Advantage** — Band-7-focused Reading & Writing strategy.
- **E2 IELTS** (YouTube) — short, sharp per-question-type strategy videos.
> **Diet rule:** one teaching source *per technique*, then practise. After Week 2 you should be ~80% practising, ~20% learning. Watching ≠ studying.

### 3. Daily Vocabulary & Grammar habit (the 60-day language engine)
**Vocabulary — collocations, not words (20 min/day):**
- Pick the day's topic from the rotation: **Health · Environment · Technology · Education · Crime · Work · Society · Globalisation · Media · Government.**
- Learn **8 collocations** (e.g. "sedentary lifestyle", "carbon footprint", "widen the skills gap") — not isolated words. Write **2 sentences** with each on an IELTS-style question.
- Store them in a spaced-repetition tool (**Anki**) or your app's vocab bank. Review yesterday's + a random old set daily.
- Use **Prompt D** (Section 3) to auto-generate each day's set.

**Grammar — one structure/day, drilled in writing (15 min/day):**
- Cycle the Band-7.5 structures: complex sentences, **relative clauses**, **conditionals**, **cause-effect** (leads to / results in), **contrast** (whereas / while), and the **passive** (essential for Task 1 processes).
- Each day, write 3 sentences using that day's structure on a real topic.
- **Kill list:** keep a running list of *your* recurring errors (from marked essays) — articles, subject-verb agreement, plurals — and drill the top one until it disappears.

**The 1-sentence model habit:** study one Band-9 sentence/day, deconstruct *why* it works, and reuse its structure in your own writing.

# SECTION 3 — CLAUDE TUTORING FRAMEWORKS (how to use your coach)

> Copy-paste these. They're also built into the app under **🎓 Prompts** (one-click copy). Fill the [brackets].

### 🅰️ Prompt A — Writing Task 2 (strict feedback)
```
You are a Senior IELTS Examiner. Mark my Academic Writing Task 2 against the
official band descriptors. Be brutally honest and examiner-strict — do not inflate.
1) Give a band (to the nearest 0.5) for EACH criterion: Task Response,
   Coherence & Cohesion, Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range & Accuracy — plus overall.
2) Under each criterion, 2–3 bullets on what cost me marks, with exact quotes from my essay.
3) My top 3 recurring errors + the single highest-impact fix.
4) Rewrite my weakest paragraph at Band 8 and explain what you changed.

QUESTION: [paste the essay prompt]
MY ESSAY: [paste your essay]
```

### 🅱️ Prompt B — Writing Task 1 (analysis + vocabulary rewrite)
```
You are a Senior IELTS Examiner. Analyse my Academic Writing Task 1.
1) Band per criterion (Task Achievement, Coherence & Cohesion, Lexical, Grammar) + overall.
2) Did I include a clear OVERVIEW (key features, NO data)? Quote it, or flag it missing.
3) Did I select & group key features, avoid listing every number, and avoid opinions?
4) Give a before→after table of at least 6 of my data phrases, upgraded to more
   precise and varied vocabulary.

VISUAL: [describe or paste the chart/graph/table data]
MY ANSWER: [paste your answer]
```

### 🅲 Prompt C — Reading / Listening mistake analysis
```
You are an IELTS Reading/Listening coach. For each question I got wrong:
1) Identify the question TYPE.
2) Explain why the correct answer is right and mine is wrong — quote the exact words in
   the passage/transcript that prove it (the keyword location).
3) Name the specific TRAP I fell for (distractor, missed paraphrase, FALSE vs NOT GIVEN,
   qualifier word like "all/some/never", word-limit, etc.).
4) Give one targeted rule to avoid that trap next time.
Then summarise my ERROR PATTERN and the ONE skill I should drill this week.

DATA: [paste each: question + my answer + correct answer + the relevant passage/transcript text]
```

### 🅳 Prompt D — Daily high-yield vocabulary generator
```
You are my IELTS vocabulary coach. Generate today's high-yield set for the topic: [TOPIC].
1) 8 advanced COLLOCATIONS/phrases (Band 7.5+), each with a one-line meaning and a natural
   example sentence on an IELTS-style question.
2) 3 less-common items I can use in Speaking on this topic.
3) 2 upgrade pairs: a common word → a more precise academic alternative.
4) One ready-made Task 2 thesis sentence using two of these collocations.
Keep them natural and usable — nothing an examiner would find forced.
```
