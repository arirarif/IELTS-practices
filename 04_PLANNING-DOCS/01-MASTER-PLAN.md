## 🧮 1. The Band 7 Math (memorize this)

Overall = **average of the 4 modules, rounded to the nearest 0.5**.
- An average ending in **.25 rounds UP** (e.g. 6.75 → 7.0, 7.25 → 7.5).
- So you need the four scores to **average ≥ 6.875** to safely round to 7.0.

### 🎯 Your Strategic Target Distribution

| Module | Target band | Raw score needed | Difficulty to train |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🎧 Listening | **7.5** | ~32 / 40 correct | 🟢 Fast (objective) |
| 📖 Reading | **7.5** | ~30 / 40 correct (Academic) | 🟢 Fast (objective) |
| ✍️ Writing | **6.5** | — | 🔴 Slow (skill-based) |
| 🗣️ Speaking | **6.5** | — | 🟠 Medium (skill-based) |

**Average = (7.5 + 7.5 + 6.5 + 6.5) / 4 = 7.0 ✅**

> 🛡️ **The Cushion Principle:** We over-aim on Listening & Reading because they're *trainable to a near-guarantee* with drilling. That banks a safety buffer so a wobble in Writing/Speaking (the slow, subjective ones) doesn't sink you. **Even Writing 6.0 + everything else on target still gives 6.875 → 7.0.**

### Reality check on the timeline
- Cambridge estimates **~200 guided hours to climb one full band**. At 2–3 hrs/day × 70 days = **~175–210 hours.** It fits — *only if the hours are honest and focused.* No passive "watching videos" counts as study.

## 🧭 2. The 3-Phase Plan (10 weeks)

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PHASE 1 — FOUNDATION & DIAGNOSIS          Weeks 1–3         │
│ "Learn the game. Find the leaks."         Days 1–21         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ PHASE 2 — STRATEGY & DRILLING             Weeks 4–7         │
│ "Master the methods. Hammer weak points." Days 22–49        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ PHASE 3 — EXAM SIMULATION & POLISH        Weeks 8–10        │
│ "Full mocks under pressure. Sharpen. Peak." Days 50–70      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Phase 1 — Foundation & Diagnosis (Weeks 1–3 · Days 1–21)
**Goal:** Understand the *exact* format of all 4 modules + every question type, and get a **brutally honest baseline**.
- Day 1–2: Full **diagnostic mock** (timed) → real starting bands.
- Learn the structure of each module and every question type (they're finite and predictable).
- Build daily habits: spelling drills, reading-aloud, essay structure.
- **Output:** We know your real weak points. The plan gets tuned to YOU.

### Phase 2 — Strategy & Drilling (Weeks 4–7 · Days 22–49)
**Goal:** Convert knowledge → reliable technique. This is where bands are built.
- One module gets a "deep day" in rotation, weak points get extra reps.
- Reading: drill T/F/NG, Matching Headings, time-boxing (the band-killers).
- Writing: write 2–3 essays/week, every one marked examiner-strict, rewrite from feedback.
- Speaking: daily talking, weekly recorded full mock.
- Listening: daily section practice + error-pattern logging.
- **Output:** Methods become automatic. Scores climbing in the tracker.

### Phase 3 — Exam Simulation & Polish (Weeks 8–10 · Days 50–70)
**Goal:** Make exam conditions feel boring and easy.
- **2 full timed mocks/week**, computer-delivered conditions (on screen, typed Writing).
- Tighten timing, fix recurring errors, lock in templates.
- Final week: taper — light review, model answers, rest. **Arrive sharp, not fried.**
- **Output:** You walk in calm, having already "taken the test" 6+ times.

## 🪜 3. What Actually Separates Band 6 from Band 7

> From the research — Band 7 rarely fails on *ideas* or *vocabulary*. It fails on **performance control.**

| Module | The Band 6 → 7 jump is about… |
|---|---|
| 🎧 Listening | Killing careless errors: **spelling, plurals, numbers**, and not falling for **distractors** (info the speaker says then corrects). |
| 📖 Reading | **Time management** + not confusing FALSE with NOT GIVEN + reading for *meaning* not keyword-matching. |
| ✍️ Writing | A clear **overview/position**, answering the *exact* prompt, and **original phrasing** (drop memorized templates), well-organized paragraphs. |
| 🗣️ Speaking | **Speaking at length** without long pauses, paraphrasing when stuck, range of structures, fewer fillers. |

## ⏱️ 4. Daily Time Budget (2.5 hrs example)

| Block | Time | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | 15 min | Spelling/vocab drill OR read aloud |
| Main module (rotates) | 60 min | Today's focus from the schedule, **timed** |
| Second module | 45 min | Secondary focus / weak point |
| Review & log | 30 min | Mark mistakes, log patterns in tracker |

> Rotate so every module gets touched 3–4×/week. **Reading & Writing get the most reps** (hardest to fix). Speaking needs daily *talking* even if just 15 min.

---

## 📏 5. The Rules We Don't Break

1. ⏱️ **Everything timed.** Always. The clock is the real opponent.
2. 🔍 **Every mistake gets analyzed**, not just counted. *Why* did I miss it?
3. ✍️ **Every essay gets marked and rewritten.** One marked-and-rewritten essay > five unmarked ones.
4. 🗣️ **Talk every day**, out loud, in English. Record weekly.
5. 📊 **Log everything in the tracker.** What gets measured gets improved.
6. 🎯 **Perform, don't prove.** This is not an intelligence test. It's a points game with known rules. Play the rules.
7. 😴 **Protect sleep the week of the test.** A rested brain reads faster and hears better.
