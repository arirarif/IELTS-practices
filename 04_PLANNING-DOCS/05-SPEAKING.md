# 🗣️ 05 — SPEAKING PLAYBOOK

> **Target: Band 6.5.** Speaking is an 11–14 min face-to-face (or video) interview. The secret: it rewards **how you speak**, not how clever your ideas are. Talk freely, extend, paraphrase, keep flowing.

---

## 🎙️ The 3 Parts

| Part | Time | Format | What it tests |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Intro & interview** | 4–5 min | Q&A about you (home, work, hobbies, daily life) | Comfort, natural answers |
| **2 — Long turn (cue card)** | 3–4 min | 1 min prep, then **speak 1–2 min** on a topic card | Speaking at length, organization |
| **3 — Discussion** | 4–5 min | Abstract Q&A linked to Part 2 topic | Developing/justifying ideas |

---

## 📊 The 4 Marking Criteria (each = 25%)

| Criterion | Band 6.5 needs |
|---|---|
| **Fluency & Coherence** | Speak at length with only **occasional** hesitation; ideas connect logically. |
| **Lexical Resource** | A range of vocabulary; some less-common words; effective **paraphrase when stuck.** |
| **Grammatical Range & Accuracy** | A mix of simple + complex sentences; errors don't block understanding. |
| **Pronunciation** | Clear and easy to follow; accent is fine — clarity is what matters. |

---

## 🟨 GOLDEN RULES

1. 🗣️ **Speak at length.** The biggest fluency signal is *willingness to keep going.* Never answer Part 1 with one word — always give a reason or example. Aim 2–3 sentences minimum.
2. 🔁 **When you blank, paraphrase — don't freeze.** "What's the word… it's like a place where you keep money — a bank." Paraphrasing *gains* marks (lexical resource); freezing *loses* them.
3. 🚫 **Kill the fillers.** "Um, like, you know, actually" every 3 seconds wrecks fluency. A short pause to think is **better** than a string of fillers.
4. 🔗 **Use connectors naturally:** *because, for example, on the other hand, what I mean is, the reason being…* — these show coherence.
5. ❌ **Don't memorize scripted answers.** Examiners are trained to spot them and will switch the question. Sound natural, not rehearsed.
6. 😌 **It's a conversation, not an interrogation.** Smile, be yourself. Examiners *want* you to talk — give them material to mark.

---

## 🎯 Part 2 — The Cue Card Method (most coachable part)

You get a card + **1 minute to prepare** + must speak **1–2 min.** Most people run dry at 40 seconds. Fix:

### During the 1-min prep — use KEYWORD MAPPING (not full sentences)
Jot **4–6 keywords**, not a script. Use the card's bullet points as your skeleton, and lean on **W-questions** to generate content:

```
Topic: "Describe a place you like to visit"

  WHAT:   old riverside café, downtown
  WHEN:   weekends, since uni
  WHO:    with my brother
  WHY:    calm, cheap coffee, view of boats
  FEELING: relaxed, nostalgic
  EXTRA:  story — the day it rained and we...
```

### While speaking — follow the bullets, then EXTEND
- Cover each bullet on the card (they're your structure).
- **Add a short story or example** — that's what fills 2 minutes naturally.
- Keep going until the examiner stops you. **Speaking the full 2 min = a Band 7+ signal.**

---

## 🎯 Part 3 — Develop & Justify

- Questions get **abstract** ("Why do people prefer…?", "How might this change in future?").
- Formula: **Opinion → Reason → Example → (Alternative view).**
- It's fine to take a second: *"That's an interesting question. I'd say…"* — buys thinking time naturally.
- Show range: compare, speculate ("it might…"), generalize ("people tend to…").

---

## 🏋️ Daily Speaking Routine (yes, EVERY day)
- **15 min talking out loud in English**, even alone. Describe your day, narrate what you're doing, answer a random Part 1 question set.
- **1 cue card/day:** pick a topic, 1 min prep, record yourself speaking 2 min on your phone.
- **Listen back weekly** — note fillers, pauses, repeated words, grammar slips.
- **Shadowing:** play a fluent English clip, repeat right after the speaker — builds rhythm + pronunciation.
