# LISTENING Playbook — your other easy 7.5+ (point bank #2)

> Listening rewards **exposure + prediction + careful spelling**. With daily Cambridge audio
> you can lift this to 7.5–8. 30 min + transfer · 4 sections · 40 questions. Band 7 ≈ **30/40**.
> Tools: **Cambridge 16–20 audio** (scan the QR on the cover → phone) + your **app** (logging).

> ⚠️ Your app does NOT play audio — it logs scores. **The audio is in your Cambridge books.**
> Set that up Day 1 or this whole skill is blocked.

---

## The 4 sections (difficulty rises)
1. **Section 1** — everyday conversation (booking, form-filling). Easiest. Numbers, names, dates, spelling.
2. **Section 2** — monologue, social (a tour, a facility). Maps/plans common.
3. **Section 3** — academic discussion (2–4 speakers, e.g. students + tutor). Hardest tracking.
4. **Section 4** — academic lecture (one speaker, no pause). Note-taking under pressure.

## The #1 habit: PREDICT before each recording
- You get time to read questions first — **use it to predict** the answer type for each blank:
  *Is it a number? a name? a noun? plural? a date?* You'll catch the answer the moment it's said.
- **Listen for synonyms & signposts** ("however", "the main reason", "instead") — the answer
  often follows a signpost, and the wording won't match the question exactly.
- **Distractor trap:** speakers often say a wrong answer then correct it ("it's at 3… no, 3:30").
  The **last** stated value usually wins. Stay with the speaker.

## Where points leak (fix these)
- **Spelling** — computer-delivered, you type it. A right word spelt wrong = 0. Drill commonly
  misspelt words; if names are spelled out, write each letter.
- **Numbers/dates/measurements** — practise "fifteen vs fifty", phone numbers, dates, £/$/%, units.
- **Word limit** — "NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS AND/OR A NUMBER." Obey it exactly.
- **Plurals & grammar** — the answer must fit the sentence grammatically.
- **Don't fall behind** — if you miss one, *let it go* and catch the next; don't freeze.

## Active-listening training (beyond just testing yourself)
- After scoring a Cambridge section, **re-listen with the transcript** (in the book) and find
  *exactly* where you missed it and why (didn't know the word? distractor? spelling? speed?). Log it.
- Build ear-time daily: even 10 min of English podcasts/news (BBC, TED) trains your ear; but
  **test practice = Cambridge audio**, that's the priority.

## How to practise (the loop)
1. **1 Cambridge listening section/day**, timed, headphones, type answers.
2. Score → **log scores in your app** (tracks trend) → re-listen with transcript → log misses.
3. **1 full listening test/week** (all 4 sections back-to-back) for stamina + transfer practice.
4. Drill your weakest *section type* (often Section 3 or 4) extra.

## Do / Don't
✅ Predict answer types · use reading time · follow signposts · take the last corrected value · spell carefully · move on after a miss.
❌ Stare at one blank while the audio moves on · ignore word limits · skip the transcript review · practise without real audio.
