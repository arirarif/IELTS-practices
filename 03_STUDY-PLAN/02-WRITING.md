# WRITING Playbook — the battle for Band 7 (your hardest, highest-leverage paper)

> Writing is Bangladesh's weakest paper and the gate to 7. Win here by **control and clear
> argument**, not big words. Task 2 = ⅔ of the score → it gets ⅔ of your writing time.
> Tools: Simon *Writing* + Simon *Ideas*, Makkar (models), Pauline Cullen *Common Mistakes*,
> your **app** (score + Break It Down).

---

## The 4 criteria & what moves you 6.5 → 7 (memorise this)

| Criterion | What a 6 looks like | What 7 needs (your target) |
|---|---|---|
| **Task Response (TA)** | Addresses task but ideas underdeveloped / partial | **Fully answers ALL parts**, clear position throughout, each idea *extended* with explanation + example |
| **Coherence & Cohesion (CC)** | Ideas present but linking mechanical/overused | **Logical paragraphs** (1 idea each), smooth linking, clear referencing — NOT "Firstly/Secondly/Moreover" spam |
| **Lexical Resource (LR)** | Adequate vocab, some errors/repetition | **Less common words used *accurately*** + good collocation; precision > flashiness |
| **Grammar (GRA)** | Mix of simple/complex, frequent errors | **Variety of complex sentences with frequent error-free sentences** — *control* is the word |

> The 6→7 jump is almost always **CC + GRA** (control), not ideas. Your `Common Mistakes`
> book + Error Log target exactly this. Drill it daily.

## Task 2 — the only structure you need (4 paragraphs)
1. **Intro (2 sentences):** paraphrase the question + state your clear position/answer.
2. **Body 1:** one main idea → explain *why* → give a specific example → link to the question.
3. **Body 2:** second main idea → same pattern (point, explain, example, link).
4. **Conclusion (1–2 sentences):** restate your position; no new ideas.

- **250–290 words.** Under 250 = automatic penalty. Don't pad past ~300 (more errors).
- **40 minutes**, including ~5 min planning + 5 min checking. *Always* leave 5 min to proofread
  — that catches the article/tense/plural slips that cap Bangla speakers at 6.5.
- **Position must be consistent** start to finish. Pick a side and hold it.

## Task 1 (Academic) — efficient, don't over-invest (⅓ of score)
- Learn ~6 chart types from **Makkar Graphs**: line, bar, pie, table, process, map.
- Formula: **Intro** (paraphrase) → **Overview** (2 biggest trends — the most important sentence,
  examiners look for it) → **2 detail paragraphs** with specific data + comparisons.
- 150+ words, 20 min. Steal the *describing language* (rose sharply, plateaued, the majority of…)
  from Makkar/Simon. Practise ~8 total across the 8 weeks. Done.

## Bangla-speaker error hit-list (proofread for THESE every time)
- **Articles** (a/an/the) — most common L1 slip. Check every noun.
- **Subject–verb agreement & tense consistency.**
- **Singular/plural** (informations ✗, advices ✗ — uncountable).
- **Prepositions** (depend *on*, discuss ∅, married *to*).
- **Run-on sentences** — Bangla tolerates long chains; English needs full stops.
> Don't trust "I feel it's right." Use your Error Log: the same 3–4 errors recur — hunt them.

## How to practise writing (the loop)
1. Pick a Task 2 prompt (app prompt bank, or Makkar/Simon topic).
2. **Plan 5 min** (2 ideas + examples), write **30 min**, **check 5 min**.
3. Score in the **app** → read TA/CC/LR/GRA + **Break It Down** → note the lowest criterion.
4. **Rewrite ONE weak paragraph** to band 7 using the feedback (rewriting > always-new).
5. Log new errors. Next session, target that exact weakness.
- **3 essays/week.** Once (~week 3–4) get **2 essays checked by a real teacher** to confirm the
  app's bands are accurate — adjust your target if the human score differs by >0.5.

## Do / Don't
✅ Develop 2 ideas deeply · use specific examples · proofread for your error list · vary sentences accurately.
❌ Memorise whole essays/templates (penalised) · stuff "big words" you can't use · write 350+ words · use "Firstly/Secondly/In a nutshell" robotically · leave 0 minutes to check.
