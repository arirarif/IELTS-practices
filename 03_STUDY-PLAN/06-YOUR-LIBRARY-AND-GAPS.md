## What you DON'T need to buy
**Nothing.** You have full mocks, audio, technique, vocab, ideas, and error-correction
covered. Stop buying books — more books ≠ higher band. Depth + feedback does.

## ⚠️ What you DO still need (the real gaps — none are books)

1. **A way to get SPEAKING feedback (highest gap).** Pronunciation is 25% of Speaking and
   *no book and no app can hear you.* Options, best first:
   - A speaking partner / friend / tutor who'll do 2–3 mock interviews + honest feedback.
   - **Record yourself daily** on your phone (you already do speaking in the app — also
     record the audio) and self-check against Simon's Master Speaking model answers.
   - A few paid mock-speaking sessions (italki / Cambly / a local tutor) in weeks 6–7.
2. **2–3 of your essays checked by a real teacher/examiner — ONCE.** This *calibrates* whether
   your app's band estimates are accurate (±0.5). Do this around week 3–4. Cheap and decisive.
3. **Real exam-condition setup:** headphones + a strict timer + a quiet room, on a computer
   (your test is computer-delivered). Simulate it for every full mock.
4. **The Cambridge LISTENING AUDIO** — your Cam 16–20 books use QR codes / download links for
   audio. **Set this up in week 1** (scan the QR on the cover, save the audio to your phone)
   so listening practice is friction-free. Without audio these books are half-useful.
