# READING Playbook — your fastest route to a 7.5+ (point bank #1)

> Reading is pure **technique + speed + timing** — the most *trainable* skill in 8 weeks.
> 60 min · 3 passages · 40 questions. Band 7 ≈ **30/40**, Band 8 ≈ **35/40**.
> Tools: **Simon Master Reading** (method) + **Cambridge 16–20** (practice) + your **app** (drills).

---

## The golden rule: you do NOT read the passage fully first
You don't have time. Method:
1. **Skim** the passage ~2–3 min: title, first/last sentence of each paragraph → get the gist + structure.
2. **Read the questions**, then **scan** for keywords/synonyms to locate answers.
3. Answers usually come **in order** down the passage (except matching/heading types).
4. **~20 min per passage** (passage 3 is hardest — don't get stuck early). **Never leave a blank** — guess.

## Question-type tactics (each type has a method — learn it in Simon)
- **TRUE / FALSE / NOT GIVEN** (your likely weak spot):
  - **TRUE** = passage confirms it. **FALSE** = passage *contradicts* it. **NOT GIVEN** = passage is *silent* / you can't tell.
  - Trap: if you have to *reason/assume* to make it true → it's **NOT GIVEN**. Don't use outside knowledge.
- **YES / NO / NOT GIVEN** = same logic but about the *writer's opinion/claim*.
- **Matching Headings:** read the paragraph, find its *main idea* (not a detail), then match. Do these *last*. Cross off used headings.
- **Matching Information (which paragraph contains…):** scan-only; can be anywhere; time-eater — do later.
- **MCQ:** read all options; eliminate the clearly wrong; beware "true but doesn't answer the question."
- **Gap-fill / sentence / summary completion:** obey the **word limit** ("NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS"); copy spelling *exactly* from the passage; answer must be grammatically correct in the gap.

## Spelling & transfer
- Computer-delivered = you type answers. **Spelling counts** — a misspelt right answer is wrong.
- Singular/plural must match the sentence. Copy from the text precisely.

## Speed training (do this every week)
- Time **every** passage. If you're slow, the fix is usually **reading too much** — drill skim/scan, not "read faster."
- Build a **synonym sense**: the answer in the text is almost never the same word as the question
  (question "children" → text "youngsters"). Note synonym pairs you miss in your Error Log.

## How to practise (the loop)
1. App **reading drill** daily (fast feedback, per-type breakdown) for technique reps.
2. **Cambridge full reading paper** weekly, strictly 60 min, on screen.
3. Mark → for each wrong answer, identify the **question type** and **why** (misread? synonym missed?
   ran out of time? NG vs F confusion?). Log it.
4. Drill your 2 weakest *types* until accuracy >80%.

## Do / Don't
✅ Skim then scan · trust "answers in order" · obey word limits · guess every blank · watch the clock per passage.
❌ Read every word · use outside knowledge on TFNG · spend 30 min on passage 1 · leave blanks · ignore spelling.
