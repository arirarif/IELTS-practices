## 1. The honest truth (read this twice)

- **6.5 → 7.0 is the SLOWEST half-band to move.** Research on real candidates: the
  6.0–6.5 band improves the least per study hour, and the people who actually gained
  studied **~20–25 focused hours/week**. There is no shortcut. If you give this 8 weeks
  ~10 lazy hours/week, you will likely stay at 6.5. If you give it **18–22 honest hours/week
  of the *right* practice**, 7.0 is realistic.
- **Band 7 is about CONTROL, not big words.** The 6→7 gate (official) is *"clearer ideas
  with greater control and some precision."* Fewer errors, clearer arguments, smoother
  speech — NOT fancy vocabulary you can't use accurately. Stop hunting for "band 9 words."
- **You don't need 7 in everything.** Overall 7.0 = average of 4 skills rounded to nearest
  half. E.g. **L8 R7 S6.5 W6.5 = 7.0**. So you can be weaker in your hardest skill if you
  bank points in your easiest. We exploit this below.

## 3. The strategy in one picture

**Bank your points where they're cheapest, defend where they're expensive.**

1. **Reading + Listening = your point bank.** These are technique + exposure. With 8 weeks
   of daily timed practice you can realistically push both to **7–8**. This is where you
   *buy back* the half-band that Writing/Speaking will cost you.
2. **Writing = your battle.** Hardest to move, biggest weakness. Aim **6.5 → 7** by
   killing error patterns and writing clearer arguments. Even holding a solid 6.5 here is
   fine *if* Reading/Listening hit 7.5+.
3. **Speaking = fluency + pronunciation fixes.** Aim **6.5–7**. Cheap wins: stop hesitating,
   fix 3–4 Bangla-L1 sound errors, develop answers fully.

**Target score map (one realistic route to 7.0):**
`Listening 7.5 · Reading 7.5 · Writing 6.5 · Speaking 6.5 → 7.0 overall` ✅
(If Writing climbs to 7, even better — buffer.)

## 4. What to FOCUS on vs SKIP (you have no time to waste)

### ✅ DO (high ROI)
- **Daily timed practice** under exam conditions (timer ON, no pausing).
- **Writing Task 2** (it's 2/3 of the writing score) — argument structure + error reduction.
- **Reading/Listening question-type technique** (each type has a method; learn the method).
- **Error logging** — every mistake goes in one list; you drill *your* errors, not generic ones.
- **Speaking out loud daily** (record yourself; the app can't hear pronunciation — your phone can).
- **Full mocks weekly** for stamina (real test is long; you must build endurance).

### ❌ SKIP / DE-PRIORITISE (time traps)
- ❌ **General Training materials** — you're Academic. Ignore GT letters etc.
- ❌ **Memorising long word lists / "band 9 vocabulary"** — you'll misuse them (band-killer).
  Learn vocabulary *in topics* and *from your own errors* (use the app's বাংলা word bank).
- ❌ **Endless YouTube "tips" videos** — watch max 1–2 per question type, then PRACTISE.
- ❌ **Writing Task 1 obsession** — it's only ~1/3 of writing. Learn 5 chart templates once,
  practise a few, move on. Don't let it eat Task 2 time.
- ❌ **Re-reading whole reading passages slowly** — train skim/scan; you will run out of time otherwise.
- ❌ **Memorising scripted speaking answers** — examiners detect and penalise this.

**The loop:** Practise on app/book → score it → log the errors → the app's "Break It Down"
+ band descriptors tell you *why* → drill that exact weakness next day → weekly full mock to
measure. Repeat. This feedback loop is the whole game.

## 6. The weekly rhythm (target ~18–22h)

- **6 study days + 1 light/rest day.** Burnout loses more than it gains.
- Roughly per week: **Writing 5h · Reading 4h · Listening 4h · Speaking 3h · Vocab+review 2h
  · 1 full timed section/mock 2h.**
- Every day touches **at least 2 skills** (don't silo). See `01-CALENDAR-8-WEEK.md`.

## 7. What "done" looks like (success criteria)
- By **Week 4**: consistent 7+ on timed Reading & Listening sections.
- By **Week 6**: Writing Task 2 essays with <5 noticeable errors and a clear 4-paragraph argument.
- By **Week 7**: a full mock at **L7.5 R7.5 W6.5 S6.5+** under real timing.
- **Week 8**: taper — light review, mocks, sleep, exam-day logistics.
