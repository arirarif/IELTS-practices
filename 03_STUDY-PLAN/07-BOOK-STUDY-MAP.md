# The Book Study Map — what to actually study (and skip) per skill

> Written as your coach: 8 weeks, Bangla L1, 6.5 → 7.0. You will NOT finish 25 books — and you
> don't need to. Below is the exact *portion* of each book to study per skill, the priority, and
> the fastest finishing order. Rule of thumb baked in throughout:
> **Practice books you DO (timed). Method books you read ONCE and apply. Reference books you DIP into.**

---

## The 3 laws (everything obeys these)
1. **Effort vs. points are different things.** Writing & Speaking need the most *effort* (hardest to
   raise). Reading & Listening give the most *reliable, fast points*. So you do **both**: bank R+L to
   7.5, and fight hard for W to 7 / S to 6.5–7. Don't sacrifice one for the other.
2. **Control beats vocabulary.** Band 7 = fewer errors + clearer ideas, not big words.
3. **Reps with feedback > volume.** 1 essay scored + corrected + rewritten beats 5 essays ignored.

## Effort split (your ~20h/week)
| Skill | Effort | Why |
|---|---|---|
| ✍️ Writing | **30%** | Hardest, your weakest, the gate to 7 |
| 🗣️ Speaking | **20%** | Productive skill; pronunciation 25% and self-fixable |
| 📖 Reading | **20%** | Technique → reliable 7.5 (point bank) |
| 🎧 Listening | **20%** | Exposure → reliable 7.5 (point bank) |
| 📚 Vocabulary | **10%** | Woven into all of the above daily |

---

## ✍️ WRITING — your #1 effort (study these portions only)

**Primary method — Simon, *IELTS Writing Task 1 & 2* (read once, Week 1–2, then apply):**
- Study his **Task 2 method**: the 4-paragraph plan, *"think of ideas before vocabulary,"* 2-sentence
  intro, one clear main idea per body paragraph, develop by explaining + giving a specific example.
- **Actively analyse ~10–15 of his model essays** (not just read): mark how each paragraph has ONE
  idea, how he links sentences, how he answers *all* parts of the question. This is your band-7 template.
- **Task 1:** study his *overview-first* approach (the 2 biggest trends) + the data-describing language.

**Idea fuel — Simon, *Ideas for IELTS Essay Topics* (reference):**
- Don't read all 150 topics. Study the **~10 highest-frequency themes**: education, technology,
  environment, health, crime/punishment, work, globalisation, government spending, family, media.
- For each: take 2–3 ideas + the topic vocabulary. That's your "never run out of ideas" insurance.

**Control — Pauline Cullen, *Common Mistakes at IELTS* (15 min DAILY — this is your 6→7 secret):**
- Focus the units on: **articles (a/an/the), verb tense & subject–verb agreement, prepositions,
  countable/uncountable nouns, and sentence structure (run-ons).** These are exactly the Bangla-L1
  errors that cap you at 6.5. One unit a day.

**Model bank — Makkar, *Academic Essays* (dip in only):** read 1–2 model essays for a weak topic to
see structure/ideas. **Never memorise or copy** — templated language is penalised.

**Task 1 graphs — Makkar, *Graphs* (light):** learn the ~6 chart types + describing phrases; practise
~6–8 graphs total. Stop there (Task 1 = only ⅓ of writing).

**SKIP entirely:** Rachel Mitchell *Writing*, Sam McCarter *Academic Writing Practice*, all General
Training letter material.
**Practice:** 3 essays/week → score in the app → "Break It Down" → fix the lowest criterion → rewrite one paragraph.

## 📖 READING — point bank (mostly DOING, little reading)

**Method — Simon, *Master IELTS Reading* (read once, Week 1):**
- Study the **per-question-type techniques**: skim then scan, the **TRUE vs NOT GIVEN** distinction,
  matching headings (main idea, not detail), keyword→synonym hunting, and time-per-passage.

**Practice (90% of your reading prep) — Cambridge 16–20:** DO full reading papers, strictly 60 min,
on screen. Log every wrong answer **by question type**, drill your 2 weakest types.

**SKIP:** Rachel Mitchell *Reading*. Reading "theory" beyond technique. Reading whole passages slowly.

---

## 🎧 LISTENING — point bank (almost pure DOING)

**Method (minimal — 30 min total):** the 4 habits — **predict** answer types before each section,
follow **signposts**, watch **distractors** (last corrected value), and **spelling/numbers**. (Skim
only the tips section of Rachel Mitchell *Listening* if you want it written down — otherwise skip the book.)

**Practice (the whole game) — Cambridge 16–20 audio** (now embedded in your app's Listening tab via
Engnovate): 1 section/day + 1 full test/week. **Re-listen with the transcript** after scoring to find
exactly why you missed each one.

**SKIP:** Rachel Mitchell *Listening* (beyond the tips), random non-IELTS audio as "practice."

---

## 🗣️ SPEAKING — high effort, can't self-assess sound

**Method — Simon, *Master IELTS Speaking* (read once + use models):**
- Study the **Part 1 / 2 / 3 method**: P1 answer in 2–3 sentences, P2 cover all bullets & speak the
  full 2 min, P3 develop (opinion → reason → example). Use his model answers to hear how ideas extend.

**Question bank — Makkar, *Speaking Guesswork (May–Aug 2026)* (practise aloud, Week 4–8):** these are
the **likely topics for your exam window** — record yourself answering them.

**Pronunciation (25% of the score, self-fixable):** record on your phone, find your Bangla-L1 sounds
(f/v, th, z/j, ship/sheep, school→iskool, flat stress), drill the 3 worst with minimal pairs. *No book
can do this — only recording yourself + a partner/tutor can.*

**SKIP:** Rachel Mitchell *Speaking*. Memorising scripted answers (penalised).

---

## 📚 VOCABULARY — 10–15 min daily, woven in

- **Cambridge IELTS Vocabulary (English–Bangla)** — your daily driver: 10–15 words in context, in বাংলা.
- **Pauline Cullen, *Vocabulary for IELTS*** — dip into the **topic units that match common essay
  themes** (environment, education, health…) for collocations. Don't do it cover-to-cover.
- **Build from your own errors** — every unknown word from reading/listening → your app's word bank.
- **SKIP:** memorising decontextualised "band 9 word lists." You'll misuse them → band-killer.

---

## The fastest finishing order (your rules set)

**Weeks 1–2 — Learn the method (read-once phase):**
- Read Simon Reading, Simon Speaking, Simon Writing (method + a few models). Baseline full mock (Cam 16).
- Start daily: Cullen *Common Mistakes* (1 unit) + 10 vocab words.

**Weeks 3–7 — Do the work (practice phase, this is where the band moves):**
- **Cambridge:** 1 full mock/week + 1 timed section daily (rotate R/L). Log errors by type.
- **Writing:** 3 essays/week, scored + corrected + 1 paragraph rewritten. Pull ideas from Simon *Ideas*.
- **Speaking:** record daily; Week 5+ drill Makkar Guesswork topics; fix pronunciation.
- **Daily:** Cullen + vocab continue. Mid-point (Week 4) re-mock + get 2 essays human-checked.

**Week 8 — Taper:** review notes + error log + best essays. No new books. Sleep. Logistics.

## How to track (one line each)
- **Tests:** your app auto-logs Listening + Reading to History — watch the trend hit 7.5.
- **Errors:** one Error Log (error → fix → rule), re-read weekly (app's issue tags mirror this).
- **Books:** tick Simon chapters read once; tick Cambridge tests done (target: all of 16–20).

> **Bottom line:** Follow **Simon** for method, **do Cambridge 16–20** for practice, drill **Cullen**
> daily for control, fuel ideas from **Simon *Ideas* + Makkar**, and **record yourself** for speaking.
> That's ~6 books used *partially* and on purpose — not 25 books finished. That focus is what gets 7.0.
