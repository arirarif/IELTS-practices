06 · Use these, shelve those

Your Library — curated

You own ~25 IELTS books. That's more than enough — the danger is spreading thin. Here's your Core 7. Use those; shelve the rest unless a core book runs out.

Honest bottom line You are over-resourced and under-time. You don't have a "what to study" problem — you have a "study the right thing, consistently, with feedback" problem.

Why Simon over Rachel Mitchell? Simon (ex-examiner) is the gold standard for Band 7+ method. The Rachel Mitchell "…8.0+ in 10 minutes a day" series is marketing-heavy and shallow — fine for quick tips, but don't build your plan on it. One quality source per skill, go deep.

🥈 Tier 2 — use only for a specific need

  • Makkar — Academic Essays (Task 2, 400+): read 1–2 models for structure/ideas on weak topics. Don't memorise or copy (templated language caps you).
  • Makkar — Graphs (Task 1, 250+): your Task 1 source. Learn ~6 chart types + describing language; practise ~8 graphs. Don't over-invest.
  • Pauline Cullen — Vocabulary for IELTS: richer topic vocab if you want more than the Bangla book.
  • Official Cambridge Guide to IELTS: good all-skills reference + extra tests if you finish Cam 16–20.

🥉 Tier 3 — shelve for now (redundant)

  • Rachel Mitchell Speaking / Reading / Listening / Writing — superseded by Simon + Cambridge.
  • Cambridge IELTS 11–15 — keep as backup mock supply after you finish 16–20.
  • Sam McCarter Academic Writing Practice, IELTS on Track — fine but not needed alongside Simon + Cambridge.
Tip Put Tier 3 on a literal "later" shelf — it removes the daily "which book?" decision fatigue.

What you DON'T need to buy

Nothing. You have full mocks, audio, technique, vocab, ideas, and error-correction covered. More books ≠ higher band. Depth + feedback does.

⚠️ What you DO still need (none are books)

  1. Speaking feedback (biggest gap). Pronunciation is 25% and no book/app can hear you. Best → worst: a speaking partner/tutor for 2–3 mock interviews · record yourself daily & self-check vs Simon models · a few paid mock-speaking sessions (italki/Cambly) in weeks 6–7.
  2. 2–3 essays checked by a real teacher — once (~week 3–4) to calibrate whether the app's bands are accurate (±0.5).
  3. Real exam-condition setup: headphones + strict timer + quiet room, on a computer (your test is computer-delivered).
  4. The Cambridge listening audio — scan the QR on Cam 16–20 covers, save to your phone, in week 1. Without it these books are half-useful.
