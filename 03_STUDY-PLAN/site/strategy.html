Your Master Strategy

Academic · computer-delivered · L1 Bangla · target 7.0 from ~6.0–6.5 · ~8 weeks left. Tight but achievable — if you train the right things.

1. The honest truth (read twice)

  • 6.5 → 7.0 is the slowest half-band to move. The people who actually gain studied ~20–25 focused hours/week. No shortcut. ~10 lazy hrs/week → you stay at 6.5. ~18–22 honest hrs/week of the right practice → 7.0 is realistic.
  • Band 7 is CONTROL, not big words. The 6→7 gate is "clearer ideas with greater control and some precision" — fewer errors, clearer arguments. Stop hunting "band 9 words."
  • You don't need 7 in everything. Overall = average of 4 rounded to nearest 0.5. So bank points where they're cheap, lose a little where they're expensive.

Week-1 job #1 Get a REAL baseline — one full timed mock per skill (Cambridge). The app's bands are estimates for tracking, not your official score.

3. The strategy: bank cheap points, defend expensive ones

  1. Reading + Listening = your point bank. Technique + exposure → push both to 7–8. This buys back the half-band Writing/Speaking cost.
  2. Writing = your battle. Hardest to move. Aim 6.5→7 by killing error patterns and writing clearer arguments.
  3. Speaking = fluency + pronunciation. Stop hesitating, fix 3–4 Bangla-L1 sounds, develop answers fully.
Target map (one route to 7.0) Listening 7.5 · Reading 7.5 · Writing 6.5 · Speaking 6.5 → 7.0 overall ✅

4. Focus vs Skip

✅ Do (high ROI)

  • Daily timed practice (timer ON, no pausing)
  • Writing Task 2 (⅔ of the score) — argument + error reduction
  • Reading/Listening question-type technique
  • Error logging — drill your mistakes
  • Speak out loud daily & record yourself
  • Full mocks weekly for stamina

❌ Skip (time traps)

  • General Training materials — you're Academic
  • Memorising long word lists / "band 9 vocab"
  • Endless YouTube "tips" videos
  • Writing Task 1 obsession (only ⅓ of writing)
  • Re-reading whole passages slowly
  • Memorising scripted speaking answers (penalised)

6. Weekly rhythm (~18–22h)

6 study days + 1 rest. Roughly: Writing 5h · Reading 4h · Listening 4h · Speaking 3h · Vocab+review 2h · one full timed mock. Every day touches 2+ skills.

7. What "done" looks like

  • Week 4: consistent 7+ on timed Reading & Listening.
  • Week 6: Task 2 essays with <5 noticeable errors + clear 4-paragraph argument.
  • Week 7: full mock at L7.5 R7.5 W6.5 S6.5+ under real timing.
  • Week 8: taper — light review, mocks, sleep, logistics.

8. What you still need (you own the books — buy nothing)

  1. Set up the Cambridge listening audio (scan the QR on Cam 16–20) — day 1.
  2. A speaking partner OR daily self-recording — pronunciation is 25% & invisible to the app.
  3. 2–3 essays checked by a real teacher once (~week 3–4) to calibrate the app's scores.
  4. A quiet computer + headphones + timer to simulate the computer-delivered exam.
  5. Discipline & time — your true bottleneck. You're over-resourced; now execute.
