02 · Your hardest, highest-leverage paper

Writing Playbook

Win by control and clear argument, not big words. Task 2 = ⅔ of the score → ⅔ of your time. Tools: Simon Writing + Ideas, Makkar (models), Pauline Cullen Common Mistakes, your app (score + Break It Down).

The 4 criteria & the 6.5 → 7 move

CriterionA "6" looks likeWhat 7 needs
Task ResponseAddresses task, ideas underdevelopedFully answers ALL parts, clear position, each idea extended with explanation + example
Coherence & CohesionLinking mechanical/overusedLogical paragraphs (1 idea each), smooth linking — NOT "Firstly/Secondly/Moreover" spam
Lexical ResourceAdequate, some repetition/errorsLess common words used accurately + collocation; precision > flashiness
GrammarFrequent errorsVariety of complex sentences with frequent error-free sentences — control
The 6→7 jump is almost always CC + GRA (control) not ideas. Your Common Mistakes book + Error Log target exactly this. Drill daily.

Task 2 — the only structure you need (4 paragraphs)

  1. Intro (2 sentences): paraphrase the question + state your clear position.
  2. Body 1: one idea → explain why → specific example → link to question.
  3. Body 2: second idea → same pattern.
  4. Conclusion (1–2 sentences): restate position; no new ideas.
  • 250–290 words. Under 250 = automatic penalty. Don't pad past ~300.
  • 40 min = ~5 plan + 30 write + 5 check. Always proofread — it catches the slips that cap you at 6.5.
  • Hold one position start to finish.

Task 1 (Academic) — efficient, don't over-invest (⅓ of score)

  • Learn ~6 chart types from Makkar Graphs: line, bar, pie, table, process, map.
  • Formula: Intro (paraphrase) → Overview (2 biggest trends — the key sentence examiners look for) → 2 detail paragraphs with specific data + comparisons.
  • 150+ words, 20 min. Steal describing language (rose sharply, plateaued, the majority of…). Practise ~8 total. Done.

⚠️ Bangla-speaker error hit-list (proofread for THESE)

  • Articles (a/an/the) — most common L1 slip. Check every noun.
  • Subject–verb agreement & tense consistency.
  • Singular/plural (informations ✗, advices ✗ — uncountable).
  • Prepositions (depend on, discuss ∅, married to).
  • Run-on sentences — Bangla tolerates long chains; English needs full stops.

How to practise (the loop)

  1. Pick a Task 2 prompt (app bank, or Makkar/Simon topic).
  2. Plan 5 min → write 30 min → check 5 min.
  3. Score in the app → read TA/CC/LR/GRA + Break It Down → note the lowest criterion.
  4. Rewrite ONE weak paragraph to band 7 (rewriting > always-new).
  5. Log new errors; target that weakness next time.

3 essays/week. Once (~week 3–4) get 2 essays checked by a real teacher to confirm the app's bands.

✅ Do

  • Develop 2 ideas deeply
  • Use specific examples
  • Proofread for your error list
  • Vary sentences accurately

❌ Don't

  • Memorise whole essays/templates (penalised)
  • Stuff "big words" you can't use
  • Write 350+ words
  • Use "Firstly/In a nutshell" robotically
  • Leave 0 min to check
