Listening Playbook

Rewards exposure + prediction + careful spelling. With daily Cambridge audio you can reach 7.5–8. 30 min + transfer · 4 sections · 40 questions. Band 7 ≈ 30/40.

⚠️ The audio is in your BOOKS, not the app Your app logs scores; it doesn't play audio. Scan the QR on the Cam 16–20 covers → save audio to your phone on Day 1, or this whole skill is blocked.

The 4 sections (difficulty rises)

  1. Section 1 — everyday conversation (booking, form). Easiest. Numbers, names, dates, spelling.
  2. Section 2 — social monologue (tour, facility). Maps/plans common.
  3. Section 3 — academic discussion (2–4 speakers). Hardest tracking.
  4. Section 4 — academic lecture (one speaker, no pause). Note-taking under pressure.

The #1 habit: PREDICT before each recording

  • Use the question-reading time to predict the answer type for each blank — number? name? noun? plural? date? You'll catch it the moment it's said.
  • Listen for signposts ("however", "the main reason", "instead") — answers follow them, and wording won't match exactly.
  • Distractor trap: speakers say a wrong value then correct it ("3… no, 3:30"). The last value usually wins.

Where points leak (fix these)

  • Spelling — typed answers; a right word spelt wrong = 0.
  • Numbers/dates — "fifteen vs fifty", phone numbers, £/$/%, units.
  • Word limit — "NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS AND/OR A NUMBER." Obey exactly.
  • Plurals & grammar — answer must fit the sentence.
  • Don't fall behind — miss one, let it go, catch the next. Don't freeze.

Active-listening training

  • After scoring, re-listen with the transcript (in the book) and find exactly where & why you missed it. Log it.
  • Build ear-time daily (BBC/TED 10 min) — but real test practice = Cambridge audio.

How to practise (the loop)

  1. 1 Cambridge section/day, timed, headphones, type answers.
  2. Score → log in the app → re-listen with transcript → log misses.
  3. 1 full listening test/week (all 4 sections) for stamina.
  4. Drill your weakest section type (often 3 or 4).

✅ Do

  • Predict answer types
  • Use reading time
  • Follow signposts
  • Take the last corrected value
  • Spell carefully

❌ Don't

  • Stare at one blank as audio moves on
  • Ignore word limits
  • Skip the transcript review
  • Practise without real audio
