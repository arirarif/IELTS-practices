05 · The part your app can't hear

Speaking Playbook

11–14 min, 3 parts, real examiner. Scored on Fluency, Lexis, Grammar, and Pronunciation (25%). Your app/books can't hear you → record yourself / use a partner. Tools: Simon Master Speaking, Makkar Speaking Guesswork (May–Aug 2026), your phone.

The 4 criteria & the 6→7 move

CriterionWhat 7 needs
Fluency & CoherenceSpeak at length without long pauses or self-correction. Hesitation is the #1 band-killer.
Lexical ResourceSome less-common + idiomatic language, used naturally; paraphrase well.
GrammarRange of structures with frequent error-free sentences; control under pressure.
PronunciationClear & understandable throughout; good stress/intonation. Bangla-L1 sounds cost points — see below.

The format & method

  • Part 1 (4–5 min): familiar topics. Answer 2–3 sentences — not one word, not a speech.
  • Part 2 (long turn): 1 min prep, speak 1–2 min. Cover all bullets, keep going, tell it like a story. Practise this most.
  • Part 3 (4–5 min): abstract discussion. Develop: opinion → reason → example → other side. This decides 6 vs 7.
Golden rule Fluent + slightly-imperfect beats perfect-but-hesitant. Keep talking.

⚠️ Bangla-L1 pronunciation — your targeted fixes

Common Bangla-speaker patterns (not everyone has all). Record yourself, find which YOU do, drill only those with minimal pairs (5 min/day).

Cheap points Pronunciation is 25%. Pick your 3 worst sounds, 5 min/day of minimal pairs + record + compare to a native model (YouTube "minimal pairs [sound]").

How to practise (record EVERY time)

  1. Take questions from Makkar Speaking Guesswork (May–Aug 2026) — likely your real topics.
  2. Answer out loud + record on your phone (also use the app for grammar/vocab feedback).
  3. Play back: count pauses/fillers, catch grammar slips, check your Bangla-L1 sounds.
  4. Re-record the same answer with the fixes — hear the improvement.
  5. Weekly: a full mock with a partner/tutor (or self-simulate all 3 parts).

Quick wins (do now)

  • Fluency fillers that buy time naturally: "That's an interesting question…", "I'd say that…", "It depends, but generally…" (not "um").
  • Have flexible opinions ready for common Part 3 themes (technology, education, environment, work) — from Simon Ideas.
  • Smile, slow down slightly, breathe. Nervous fast speech wrecks pronunciation.

✅ Do

  • Keep talking
  • Develop with reasons + examples
  • Record & self-correct
  • Drill YOUR sound errors
  • Cover all Part 2 bullets

❌ Don't

  • Memorise scripted answers (penalised)
  • Give one-word answers
  • Go silent hunting "the perfect word"
  • Ignore pronunciation
