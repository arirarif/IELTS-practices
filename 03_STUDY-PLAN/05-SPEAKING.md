# SPEAKING Playbook — fluency + pronunciation (the part your app can't hear)

> 11–14 min, 3 parts, with a real examiner. Scored on **Fluency & Coherence, Lexical Resource,
> Grammar, and Pronunciation (25%)**. Your app + books *cannot hear you* → you MUST record
> yourself / use a partner. Tools: **Simon Master Speaking** (method), **Makkar Speaking
> Guesswork May–Aug 2026** (likely your questions), your phone (record), the app (transcript practice).

---

## The 4 criteria & the 6→7 move
| Criterion | 7 needs |
|---|---|
| **Fluency & Coherence** | Speak at length **without long pauses or self-correction**; ideas connected. Hesitation is the #1 band-killer. |
| **Lexical Resource** | Some less-common vocab + idiomatic language used naturally; paraphrase well. |
| **Grammar** | Range of structures with **frequent error-free sentences**; control under pressure. |
| **Pronunciation** | Clear, can be understood throughout; good stress/intonation. **Bangla-L1 sounds cost points — see below.** |

## The format & method (from Simon)
- **Part 1 (4–5 min):** familiar topics (home, work, hobbies). Answer **2–3 sentences** — not one
  word, not a speech. Be natural.
- **Part 2 (long turn):** 1 min to prep, **speak 1–2 min** on a cue card. **Cover all bullet
  points**, keep going, tell it like a little story. Practise this most — it's where fluency shows.
- **Part 3 (4–5 min):** abstract discussion linked to Part 2. **Develop** answers: opinion →
  reason → example → (other side). This is where 7 vs 6 is decided.
- **Golden rule:** fluent + slightly-imperfect **beats** perfect-but-hesitant. Keep talking.

## ⚠️ Bangla-L1 pronunciation — your targeted fix list (research-backed)
These are *common* Bangla-speaker patterns (not everyone has all). **Record yourself and check
which ones YOU do**, then drill those with minimal pairs. Per-learner — don't drill what you don't have.

| Pattern | Example | Minimal-pair drill |
|---|---|---|
| /f/ → /pʰ/ , /v/ → /bʱ/ | "very" → "bheri", "fan" → "phan" | fan/pan · very/berry · vote/boat |
| /θ/, /ð/ (th) → /t/, /d/ | "think" → "tink", "this" → "dis" | think/tink · they/day · three/tree |
| /z/ ↔ /dʒ/ swap | "zoo" → "joo", "zero" → "jero" | zoo/Jew · zip/jeep · zest/jest |
| Lost vowel-length contrast | "full" = "fool", "ship" = "sheep" | ship/sheep · full/fool · bit/beat |
| Vowel inserted in clusters | "school" → "iskool", "street" → "istreet" | say s-t-r as one blend; slow then speed up |
| Flat word stress / syllable-timed | every syllable equal | learn word stress (PHOto-graph vs pho-TO-graphy); stress content words, weaken the rest |

**Drill method:** pick your 3 worst, do 5 min/day of minimal pairs out loud + record + compare to a
native model (YouTube "minimal pairs [sound]"). Pronunciation is 25% — these fixes are cheap points.

## How to practise (the loop) — record EVERY time
1. Take questions from **Makkar Speaking Guesswork (May–Aug 2026)** — likely your real topics.
2. Answer **out loud** + **record on your phone**. (Also use the app's speaking flow for the
   transcript-based feedback on grammar/vocab — but the audio check is on you.)
3. Play it back: count **pauses/fillers** ("um", "you know"), catch grammar slips, check your
   Bangla-L1 sounds. Note 1–2 fixes.
4. **Re-record the same answer** applying the fixes — hear the improvement.
5. Weekly: a **full mock** with a partner/tutor (or self-simulate all 3 parts back-to-back).

## Quick wins (do these now)
- Build **fluency fillers** that buy thinking time naturally: *"That's an interesting question…",
  "I'd say that…", "It depends, but generally…"* (not "um").
- Have **flexible opinions** ready for common Part 3 themes (technology, education, environment,
  work) — from Simon *Ideas* — so you never freeze.
- Smile, slow down slightly, breathe. Nervous fast speech wrecks pronunciation.

## Do / Don't
✅ Keep talking · develop with reasons+examples · record & self-correct · drill YOUR sound errors · cover all Part 2 bullets.
❌ Memorise scripted answers (examiners detect → penalty) · give one-word answers · go silent to "find the perfect word" · ignore pronunciation because the app can't grade it.
