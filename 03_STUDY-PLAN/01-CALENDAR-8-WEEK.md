# The 8-Week Calendar (15 Jun → ~13 Aug 2026)

> Built around **your Core 7 books** (see `06`) + **your IELTS Coach app**.
> 6 study days + 1 rest day per week. Pick your time tier and protect the hours like an exam.

---

## Time tiers — be honest about which you'll actually do
| Tier | Hours/week | Realistic outcome for 6.5→7 in 8 weeks |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum | ~10–12h | Risky. Maybe holds 6.5; 7 unlikely unless you're closer than you think. |
| **Recommended** ⭐ | **~18–22h** | **7.0 is realistic** if focused on the right things. Aim here. |
| Aggressive | ~25–30h | Strong shot at 7+; needed if your baseline mock is below 6.0. |

> Decide your tier after the **Week-1 baseline mock**. If baseline is ~6.0 → go Aggressive.
> If ~6.5 → Recommended is enough.

---

## The repeatable DAILY template (~3 hours, Recommended tier)
Do this most study days. Always **2+ skills/day**, always with a **timer**.

| Block | Time | What | Tool |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Warm-up vocab** | 15 min | 10 new words in context (topic-based) + review yesterday's | Cambridge Vocab (Bangla) + **app /vocab** |
| **B. Main skill (rotate)** | 60 min | One timed Reading passage OR one Listening section OR one essay — under exam timing | Cambridge 16–20 + **app** |
| **C. Feedback + error log** | 30 min | Mark it. Find WHY each error happened. Add to your one Error Log. App "Break It Down". | **app** + answer key |
| **D. Control drill** | 15 min | One unit of *Common Mistakes at IELTS* (grammar/accuracy = the 6→7 gate) | Pauline Cullen |
| **E. Speaking out loud** | 30 min | Answer 3–5 questions aloud, **record yourself**, compare to Simon model | Simon/Makkar Speaking + phone |

> **The Error Log is the secret weapon.** One file/notebook. Every mistake → write the error,
> the correct version, and the rule. Re-read it weekly. You drill *your* gaps, not generic ones.
> (Your app's IssueTags + History already do this automatically — review them weekly too.)

## Week-by-week

### 🔍 WEEK 1 — Diagnose + learn the method (don't grind blindly yet)
- **Day 1:** Set up Cambridge **listening audio** (QR on cover → phone). Do a **full timed
  baseline mock** (Cam 16, all 4 papers, real timing). Score it honestly. **This sets your tier + focus.**
- **Days 2–6:** Learn technique, one skill/day: Simon *Master Reading* (skim/scan, each
  question type) · Simon *Master Speaking* (Part 1/2/3 method) · Simon *Writing* (Task 2
  structure) · one Task 1 chart type from Makkar Graphs · Listening section types.
- Start the **Error Log** + daily vocab. Tell your app your real bands (Settings/exam date).
- **End of week:** you know your weakest skill and the method for each. 

### 🛠 WEEKS 2–3 — Build technique into habit (highest-leverage grind)
- Daily template every day. **Rotate skills** so each gets ~2 focused sessions/week.
- **Writing:** 3 essays/week (Task 2 ×2, Task 1 ×1). Score in the app → Break It Down →
  fix the criterion that's lowest (usually CC or GRA for 6→7). Use Simon *Ideas* when stuck.
- **Reading/Listening:** 1 timed section daily; log every wrong answer by *question type*.
- **Speaking:** record 3×/week; check fluency (no long pauses) + Bangla-L1 sounds (see `05`).
- **End Week 3:** do a **half-mock** (Reading + Listening timed). Get **2 essays checked by a
  real teacher** to calibrate the app's scores.

### 📊 WEEK 4 — Mid-point full mock + course-correct
- **Full timed mock (Cam 17).** Compare to Week-1 baseline. Where did you move? Where stuck?
- Rewrite your focus for the second half around the **2 weakest question types** and the
  **lowest writing criterion**. Honest reset — half your time left.

### 🔥 WEEKS 5–6 — Intensive, exam-conditions practice
- **1 full mock/week** (Cam 18, then 19) under strict conditions (computer, headphones, timer).
- Daily: timed sections in your 2 weakest areas + 3 essays/week.
- **Speaking:** now drill **Makkar Speaking Guesswork (May–Aug 2026)** — these are likely
  *your* exam questions. Record full Part 2 long-turns (speak 2 min without stopping).
- Keep killing Error-Log patterns with *Common Mistakes* drills. Vocab daily.

### 🎯 WEEK 7 — Peak + simulate the real day
- **2 full mocks** (Cam 20 + a Cam 11–15 spare) in full exam simulation, same time of day as your real test.
- Target hit: **L7.5 R7.5 W6.5+ S6.5+**. If a skill lags, last hard push there.
- Final speaking mock with a partner/tutor if possible.

### 😴 WEEK 8 — Taper (don't cram — sleep wins points)
- Light: re-read Error Log, Simon notes, your best essays, vocab review. 1 short timed section/day max.
- **No new material.** Confirm exam logistics (ID, location/time, computer familiarity).
- Sleep well, especially the last 2 nights. Walk into the test calm and rehearsed.

## Weekly skill-time target (Recommended tier ≈ 20h)
Writing **5h** · Reading **4h** · Listening **4h** · Speaking **3h** · Vocab+ErrorLog **2h** · (weekly full mock counts within these).

> Print this. Tick each day. Consistency > intensity. A missed day is fine; a missed week isn't.
> Open the skill playbooks next: `02-WRITING` · `03-READING` · `04-LISTENING` · `05-SPEAKING`.
