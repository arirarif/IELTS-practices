## 📖 PASSAGE (transcription)

**A.** The tourism industry has explored the ancient culture of the Zulu, an African ethnic group of 11 million people who live in Kwa Zulu – Natal Province. The Zulu traditions and the culture are a great tourist attraction. Zulu are hospitable and amiable. Their language is derived from Bantu. In antiquity, they migrated into South Africa from the Niger River Delta. The tribal story tellers narrate that the Zulu are people of heaven, descended from Nguni people. One of the early settlers was Malandela, whose wife Nozinga bore him two sons; the second was named Zulu. Under the powerful reign of Shaka, all small Zulu clans were united into a proud and powerful nation. They played a significant role subsequently in the history of South Africa. Rural Zulu people live in villages, without electricity, and scarcity of water is a common feature. The poor villagers make a living selling basketwork and beadwork to tourists and urban people. During each year, they hold ceremonies to revive old cultures and tradition, the "Heritage Day" being the most widely known. On this day, thousands of Zulu virgins converge at the Royal Palace and the famous 'Reed dance' is organised. The virgins fetch the reeds from the river and bring them to the Royal palace for the King who is free to choose his youngest wife.

**B.** The visitors from all over the world are charmed by the "Bead Culture" of the Zulu. Beads are an integral part and it is considered as the dominant economic activity of Zulu and a major source of their income. The women living in villages spend most of their free time in bead work and there are special institutes which teach techniques of patterns and designs of bead art. Bead culture is carried from one generation to the other generation; they design their beadwork in attractive mathematical patterns. Bracelets, headbands, necklaces are decorated with beads, they have ceremonial significance too. In the 16th century the beads were highly coveted and prized over gold and silver. Different beads convey different messages, a rectangular ornament is a token of love, black bead conveys the emotions of mourning loneliness and anguish, pink conveys poverty and blue loyalty.

**C.** Zulu wedding is of conspicuous significance and it has many forms and cultural traditions. The wedding takes place in a church amidst Zulu colourful dances in which young virgins take keen interest. Dancing, singing and drinking beer are common practices and dancing is not confined to any special occasion. They spend the whole night in dancing and singing and celebrating the festivities. Traditional dress for men consists of animal skin and feathers. When a young woman is engaged, she lets her hair grow and covers her body with a colourful cloth as a sign of respect to her future family. She wears a heavy knee length animal hide skirt. The Zulu people have a well-devised social structure; they follow etiquettes, duties, manners, respect elders and look after the sick and the poor. It is the mother's duty to bring up the children and teach them respect and their place within the family. Young boys look after the cattle and work in the fields. They indulge in cultural games and learn stick fighting, throwing spear and rat hunting. The men defend the families' land and cattle. Zulu social structure is thus homely, simple and without any complexity.

**D.** Zulu people are by nature very orthodox and superstitious. The Sangoma has to fulfil a demand for "Umuthi" (medicine) to prevent misfortune. Sangoma is not a witch doctor, but a spiritual healer and is much respected in Zulu tribe. He works with herbs, roots, snake skins and many unusual things. A Sangoma can reveal the past, has the spiritual power to look into the future, can find the lost things and has the power to identify thieves. No one can become a Sangoma by profession; the ancestor's call makes a Sangoma. Like most African tribes, the Zulu pay tribute to the youth of the dead, they believe that the ancestors are guardian angels; they make sacrifices and perform many ceremonies to keep them happy. The Zulu men communicate with them as they are the head of the family. The Zulus believe that all diseases, misfortune, unexplained death occur by witchcraft. A Sangoma should be consulted to restore peace in the life of the family.

**E.** Zulu music is very popular; it incorporates rhythm, melody and harmony. Zulu music has been carried worldwide by Paul Simon Johnny Clegg. Zulu group Ladysmith Black has won international recognition and the "Graceland" album received two Grammy Awards. Many tourists visit the Zulu land to enjoy cultural ceremonies of Zulu people, to attend Zulu fair, dances and Zulu games.

### ব্যাখ্যা (যেখানে ফাঁদ)
- **Q4 (TRUE):** "prized over gold and silver" = সোনা-রুপার চেয়ে বেশি মূল্যবান/জনপ্রিয় ছিল → statement মেলে।
- **Q6 (FALSE):** "well-devised" (সুপরিকল্পিত) ↔ statement "carelessly planned" (এলোমেলো) — মেরু বিপরীত → **FALSE**, NG নয়।
- **Q8 (NOT GIVEN):** passage বলছে নির্দিষ্ট শিল্পী/গ্রুপ আন্তর্জাতিক স্বীকৃতি পেয়েছে; কিন্তু "**most** of the Zulu music" — এই পরিমাণ-দাবির সমর্থন নেই। ⚠️ "some recognition" দেখে TRUE দিও না; "most" passage বলেনি → **NG**।
