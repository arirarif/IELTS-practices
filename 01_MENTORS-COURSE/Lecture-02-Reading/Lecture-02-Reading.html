🅴 Matching Headings — ৬ ধাপ

১) আগে paragraph পড়ো (প্রথম+শেষ বাক্য)। ২) এক বাক্যে এর main idea বলো। ৩) তালিকা থেকে সবচেয়ে মিলে যাওয়া heading বাছো। ৪) ব্যবহৃত heading কেটে দাও। ৫) Example দেওয়া heading বাদ। ৬) বাকিগুলো elimination।

🅵 Matching Information — ৫ ধাপ

১) statement-এর "তথ্য-ধরন" বুঝো (কারণ/তুলনা/সীমাবদ্ধতা/মত)। ২) এটাকে paraphrase ধরো। ৩) পুরো passage scan করো। ৪) "any letter more than once" — একই para বারবার আসতে পারে। ৫) ক্রমে আসে না।

🆃 True/False/Not Given · Yes/No/Not Given — মূল কৌশল

তিন-প্রশ্ন টেস্ট keyword scan করে জায়গা পাও → তারপর জিজ্ঞেস করো: passage কি statement নিশ্চিত করছে? → TRUE/YES। passage কি উল্টো বলছে? → FALSE/NO। passage কি চুপ? → NOT GIVEN।
TRUE/FALSE বনাম YES/NO TRUE/FALSE/NOT GIVEN = passage-এর তথ্য যাচাই। YES/NO/NOT GIVEN = লেখকের মত/claim যাচাই। কৌশল একই; শুধু "তথ্য না মত" মাথায় রাখো।
⚠️ FALSE ≠ NOT GIVEN FALSE = passage সরাসরি উল্টোটা বলছে (contradiction)। NOT GIVEN = passage ঐ বিষয়ে কিছুই বলছে না। টেস্ট: "passage কি উল্টোটা প্রমাণ করে?" — না হলে (চুপ থাকলে) Not Given।

Question Type 5A

A Matching Headings to Paragraphs

Behind the Scenes at the Museum — Sections A–I, ১৩টা heading থেকে ৯টা বাছো। মূল কৌশল: প্রতিটা অনুচ্ছেদের topic sentence ধরো।

📖 Passage · "Behind the Scenes at the Museum" (সারসংক্ষেপ)

A. Smithsonian (1938) ran out of space; restoring specimens was a monumental task — 30 million items and seven years later, done.

B. Smithsonian owns 130 million; London 80 million; Science Museum 300,000. On an international scale, such numbers are not exceptional.

C. Collections result from the dual functions: on the one hand, public display; on the other, research.

D. Blythe House holds dangerous items — Crookes' radioactive, contaminated equipment.

E. Curator Neil Brown finds it hard to identify what future generations will regard as significant (videocassette, computer).

F. Public is surprised: he turns down antique instruments but is happy to receive a coffee maker, a green beer bottle.

G. Natural history museums collect for type specimens and for age — two reasons (moth/natural-selection study).

H. The value of collections increases — genetic analysis, pharmaceutical, bio-mimetics.

I. Collections grow bigger; space becomes a problem → out-of-town storage.

📋 নির্দেশ Section A–I প্রতিটার জন্য নিচের তালিকা (i–xiii) থেকে সঠিক heading বাছো — মোট ১৩টা থেকে ৯টা লাগবে (৪টা ফাঁদ)। আগে নিজে করো, পরে reveal খোলো।
📚 List of Headings
  1. i — An unexpected preference for modern items
  2. ii — Two distinct reasons for selection in one type of museum
  3. iii — The growing cost of housing museum exhibits
  4. iv — The growing importance of collections for research purposes
  5. v — The global 'size' of the problem
  6. vi — Why some collections are unsafe
  7. vii — Why not all museums are the same
  8. viii — The need to show as much as possible to visitors
  9. ix — How unexpected items are dealt with
  10. x — The decision-making difficulties of one curator
  11. xi — The two roles of museums
  12. xii — Who owns the museum exhibits?
  13. xiii — A lengthy, but necessary task

2Bvসংখ্যা + international scale = global 'size'
3Cxidual functions = two roles

Extra · "Shop till you drop" (A–E)

📖 Passage (সারসংক্ষেপ)

A. Mall = craze… went global (1950s) → এখন fewer and fewer people, few opened since 2006।

B. Austrian-born immigrant-এর solution: recreate… city-centre experience in suburbs।

C. my own local mall ran beauty pageants, fashion parades… more recently celebrities।

D. Always a supermarket; mall learned a great deal from supermarkets।

E. Malls spy on customers, cameras, studies of behaviour।

📋 নির্দেশ Paragraph A–E প্রতিটার জন্য নিচের তালিকা (i–vii) থেকে সঠিক heading বাছো — ২টা ফাঁদ থাকবে।
📚 List of Headings
  1. i — How one mall has promoted itself over the years
  2. ii — Reasons for government support of malls
  3. iii — Ongoing research in the psychology of shoppers
  4. iv — How malls have gone in and out of fashion
  5. v — How different countries interpret malls in new ways
  6. vi — The ideas behind the original malls
  7. vii — The influence one type of shop has had on malls
উত্তর ও ব্যাখ্যা দেখাও
1 · A iv (in & out of fashion)2 · B vi (ideas behind original)3 · C i (one mall's promotion) 4 · D vii (influence of one shop)5 · E iii (research on shoppers)

⚠️ ব্যবহৃত হয়নি: ii (government support), v (different countries) — passage-এ নেই, তাই ফাঁদ।

Question Type 5B

B Matching Information to Paragraphs

মনে রাখো statement = একটা ছোট লেবেল (compromise / restriction / comparison / benefit / warning)। হুবহু শব্দ নয়, ধারণা খোঁজো। উত্তর ক্রমে আসে না; একই paragraph একাধিকবার আসতে পারে।

"Leonardo's lost mural" (A–F)

📖 Passage (সারসংক্ষেপ)

A. Da Vinci tried a new oil painting technique, but it was not very successful; never completed।

B. One source describes Vasari as a Leonardo fan who couldn't bring himself to destroy the work।

C. Seracini spent around 40 years; clue: rather than doing what had been asked, Vasari built a false wall।

D. Drilling outcry; Martin Kemp… who wasn't involved said damage "can be repaired invisibly"।

E. Permission to continue but not in the 14 spots; only into existing cracks (a compromise)।

F. Pigment similar to those found in Mona Lisa; but only permitted… within a very narrow time period → cannot say anything definite।

📋 নির্দেশ নিচের তথ্যগুলো কোন paragraph-এ (A–F) আছে? NB: একই letter একাধিকবার ব্যবহার করতে পারো। আগে নিজে করো, পরে reveal।
  1. a compromise that allowed work to continue
  2. a connection that lends weight to a theory
  3. a report that suggests great professional respect
  4. a restriction that prevented a conclusion from being reached
  5. evidence of instructions not being followed
  6. a long-term commitment to an investigation
  7. an experiment that failed to produce satisfactory results
  8. an independent opinion on a contentious issue
উত্তর ও ব্যাখ্যা দেখাও
QStatementPara
1a compromise that allowed work to continueE
2a connection that lends weight to a theoryF
3a report suggesting great professional respectB
4a restriction that prevented a conclusionF
5evidence of instructions not being followedC
6a long-term commitment to an investigationC
7an experiment that failedA
8an independent opinion on a contentious issueD

🧠 C ও F দুবার করে — "use any letter more than once"। একটা para পেয়ে থেমো না।

Extra · "Are germs bad?" (A–D)

📖 Passage · "Are germs bad?" (সারসংক্ষেপ)

A. H. pylori is tiny — three microns; to give a sense of scale, a grain of sand is about three hundred microns long. But it would be a mistake to assume from its diminutive proportions that the bacteria is a benign presence.

B. Antibiotic medications could free the body of the bacterium and thus cure various illnesses including gastritis and stomach ulcers.

C. Professor Blaser's laboratory developed the original blood analysis techniques to identify the bacterium. Blaser engages with different intellectual activities (even a literary-criticism magazine) — perhaps this diversity of perspective first made him wonder about H. pylori.

D. In 1998, his findings appeared in the British Medical Journal; the paper concluded H. pylori might actually help promote human health, such as by regulating the level of acidity in the stomach.

📋 নির্দেশ নিচের তথ্যগুলো কোন paragraph-এ (A–D) আছে? NB: একই letter একাধিকবার ব্যবহার করতে পারো।
  1. the first test to determine the presence of H. pylori
  2. details of a pioneering academic publication
  3. one man's range of interests leading to a new approach
  4. a warning about underestimating the importance of H. pylori
  5. a medical benefit attributed to the presence of H. pylori
  6. a comparison with a natural substance familiar to most people
  7. medical problems caused by H. pylori being present
উত্তর ও ব্যাখ্যা দেখাও
QStatementPara
1first test to detect H. pyloriC (original blood analysis)
2a pioneering academic publicationD (1998 BMJ paper)
3range of interests → new approachC (diversity of perspective)
4warning against underestimating itA ("mistake to assume… benign")
5a medical benefit of its presenceD (regulates acidity)
6comparison with a familiar natural substanceA (grain of sand)
7medical problems it causesB (gastritis, ulcers)

🧠 A, C, D প্রতিটা দুবার।

Question Type 6

C True / False / Not Given · Yes / No / Not Given

"The Frog Population" (T / F / NI)

📖 Passage (key lines)

"All over the world, and even in remote areas, frogs are losing the battle… biologists are at a loss to explain." "those slimy… sometimes poisonous." "development of once-natural marshland." "frogs keep pestilent insects at manageable levels." "Discovered in 1981, less than 10 years later they vanished." "depletion of the ozone layer, a well-documented phenomenon." "Another theory is that worldwide temperature increases are upsetting breeding cycles."

📋 নির্দেশ নিচের প্রতিটা statement passage অনুযায়ী TRUE / FALSE / NOT INFORMATION (NI)? আগে নিজে লেখো, পরে reveal খোলো।
  1. Frogs are only disappearing from city areas.
  2. Frogs and toads are usually poisonous.
  3. Biologists are unable to explain why frogs are dying.
  4. Their natural habitat is becoming more and more developed.
  5. Attempts are being made to halt the development of marshland.
  6. Frogs are important because they control pests.
  7. The platypus frog became extinct by 1991.
  8. Frogs usually give birth in an underwater nest.
  9. There is convincing evidence that the ozone layer is being depleted.
  10. It is a fact that breeding cycles are upset by temperature increases.
উত্তর ও ব্যাখ্যা দেখাও
1 F2 F3 T4 T5 NI 6 T7 T8 NI9 T10 F

1 F: "only city" ↔ "all over the world/remote"। 2 F: "usually" ↔ "sometimes"। 5 NI: থামানোর চেষ্টার কথা নেই। 7 T: 1981 + <10 = ১৯৯১-এর আগে। 8 NI: "in the water" আছে, কিন্তু "nest"/"usually" নেই। 10 F: "theory" কে "fact" বলা = False।

"Staff Value a Career Path above Salary" (TRUE/FALSE/NOT GIVEN)

📖 Passage (key lines)

"retail sector is twice as high as the national average… staff turnover at 26 per cent… three out of four retailers… measures to retain people." "put a higher salary second to the chance of career progression." "70 per cent… listed training as their primary solution." "turnover was 33 per cent last year… confident morale is rising." "Managers attend… together with shop-floor staff who are earmarked for promotion."

📋 নির্দেশ নিচের প্রতিটা statement passage অনুযায়ী TRUE / FALSE / NOT GIVEN? মনে রাখো — passage উল্টো বললে F, চুপ থাকলে NG।
  1. Employees say a career path is more important than money.
  2. Staff turnover in the UK in general is 26 per cent.
  3. Retailers are attempting to keep their staff.
  4. Most employers in the survey prefer training as a way to keep staff.
  5. Tesco has reduced its staff turnover.
  6. Managers in each Tesco store designed the training programme.
  7. All Tesco employees take part in Project Future.
উত্তর ও ব্যাখ্যা দেখাও
1 TRUE2 FALSE3 TRUE4 TRUE5 NOT GIVEN6 NOT GIVEN7 FALSE

2 FALSE: 26% = retail; "UK in general" নয় (national average অর্ধেক)। 5 NG: morale বাড়া ≠ turnover কমা। 6 NG: কে design করেছে বলা নেই। 7 FALSE: "All" ↔ শুধু "earmarked for promotion"।

Extra · "Shopping for what we need or what we want?" (TRUE/FALSE/NOT GIVEN)

📖 Passage (key lines)

"Over the past hundred years, shopping has grown to become one of the leading pastimes." "an increasing proportion of populations in rich and developing nations alike… not necessarily restricted to the most affluent." "for many families, it is no longer eating together but shopping that bonds them." "it is an issue that merits serious study." "Advertising works by focusing on the promotion of our ego… rather than on specific objective facts." "The 'feel-good factor'… can be explained in these terms rather than price or value." "Companies… don't sell products, they sell lifestyles, regardless of whether they are selling a cheap daily necessity or a special luxury item." "we buy into an image of ourselves that is defined by what we have, not who we are."

📋 নির্দেশ নিচের প্রতিটা statement passage অনুযায়ী TRUE / FALSE / NOT GIVEN?
  1. Shopping has only recently become a social pleasure.
  2. Interest in shopping crosses social and national borders.
  3. In most families, mealtimes have been replaced by shopping.
  4. Medical research is being conducted into excessive shopping.
  5. An advertisement's success relies on more than the product itself.
  6. Buying things gives satisfaction as long as it is value for money.
  7. Cheaper products are easier to sell without sophisticated techniques.
  8. Our possessions make statements about our identity.
উত্তর ও ব্যাখ্যা দেখাও
1 FALSE2 TRUE3 NOT GIVEN4 NOT GIVEN5 TRUE6 FALSE7 FALSE8 TRUE

1 FALSE: "only recently" ↔ "over the past hundred years"। 3 NG: "most" ↔ passage "many"। 4 NG: "merits study" ≠ "research being conducted"। 6 FALSE: feel-good = ego, "rather than price or value"। 7 FALSE: সস্তা/দামি দুটোই একই lifestyle-কৌশলে বিক্রি।

Watch for these

🚩 NOT GIVEN-এর সংকেত (passage চুপ)

❌ তোমার (Bangla L1) Common ৮ ভুল

#ভুলএড়ানোর উপায়
1FALSE ও NOT GIVEN গুলিয়ে ফেলা"passage কি উল্টোটা বলছে?" হ্যাঁ=F, চুপ=NG
2common sense দিয়ে উত্তরশুধু লেখা আছে যা; যুক্তি নয়
3heading-এ same-word দেখে বসানোশব্দ নয়, পুরো ভাব মেলাও
4heading = একটা detail ধরাheading পুরো অনুচ্ছেদ ঢাকছে কিনা দেখো
5quantifier না মেলানোsome/most/all মিলিয়ে দেখো
6theory কে fact ভাবাtheory/may/suggests = অনিশ্চিত
7Matching Info ক্রমে খোঁজাক্রমে আসে না; পুরো passage scan
8একই para দুবার ভুলে যাওয়া"more than once" থাকলে বারবার চেক
🎯 তোমার Priority Focus বেশি জোর: (1) FALSE vs NOT GIVEN আলাদা করা · (2) Topic-sentence ধরে দ্রুত heading · (3) quantifier + negative word-এ থামা।
কম জোর: প্রতিটা অনুচ্ছেদ পুরো বোঝা — heading-এর জন্য শুধু gist লাগে।
