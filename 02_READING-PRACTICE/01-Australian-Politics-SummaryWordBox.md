## 🎯 METHOD — Summary-with-Word-Box (the rules of THIS type)

1. **Read the instruction.** "Choose from the Word List" → use the EXACT word, no form change. There are MORE words than gaps (23 vs 10) → the extra words are deliberate **traps**.
2. **Classify the box by GRAMMAR first** (noun / verb / adjective / adverb) *before* reading the passage. A gap needing an adverb can only take an adverb — instantly kills most options.
   - Adverbs: recently, now
   - Adjectives: devoted, key, leaning, Liberal, Labor, Green
   - Verbs: influence, support
   - Nouns: supporters, opportunities, welfare, liberty, money, majority, community, politicians, attention, voters, policies, trade unions, general public, support, influence
   - (`influence` & `support` are noun AND verb = trap.)
3. **Predict the part of speech of each gap** from the words around it, before looking at the box.
4. **Scan for the parallel (paraphrased) sentence** in the passage; the summary follows passage order. Pick the box-word that is the SYNONYM of the passage word.
5. **Eliminate** each used word. Each word is used once.
6. **Trap rule:** a word appearing in the passage is NOT automatically the answer. Word presence ≠ correct answer.

## 🔬 GAP-BY-GAP

- **(1) verb** — passage "is *dominated by*" → **influence** (trap: support).
- **(2) adverb** — "*in recent years*" → **recently** (trap: now).
- **(3) adjective** — "remain *faithful* … for life" → **devoted**.
- **(4) party name** — "The *Labor Party* … welfare society" → **Labor**.
- **(5) noun** — "*trade unions* political representation" → **trade unions**.
- **(6) noun** — "welfare *society*" → **community**.
- **(7) party name** — "whereas… The *Liberal Party*, on the other hand" → **Liberal**.
- **(8) noun** — "allow more *freedom* to create wealth" → **liberty**.
- **(9) noun** — "turning their *attention* to" / "pay attention" → **attention**.
- **(10) adjective** — "one *major* current issue" → **key**.

**Distractor words (do NOT pick):** supporters, opportunities, welfare, money, majority, politicians, voters, policies, general public, support, Green, leaning, now.

## 💬 PHRASES & IDIOMS

- **tell apart** = distinguish (পার্থক্য করা)
- **lean towards** = favour / be inclined to
- **for life** = forever
- **a slice of the economic pie** = a share of the wealth (idiom: slice of the pie)
- **kept in check** = controlled, restrained (নিয়ন্ত্রণে রাখা)
- **on the other hand** = contrast linker
- **make nonsense of** = ruin / render meaningless
- **free-for-all** = unregulated, anything-goes situation
- **it is no wonder that…** = it is not surprising that…
- **badly catered for** = poorly provided for / neglected
- **a swag of policies** = a bunch/collection of policies (Australian informal)
- **in theory ↔ in practice** = idea vs. reality
